Tokens

Monster Tokens are monsters that appear on the field as the result of a card's effect, for example "Scapegoat"[1].
  • They are not included in the Deck, and cannot be sent anywhere other than the field, such as the hand or Graveyard[1].
  • When a Token is destroyed or returned to the hand or Deck, they are simply removed from the field instead[1]. If Monster Tokens leave the field, they do not go to the Graveyard and are removed from the Duel[2].
  • Each Monster Token takes up 1 of your 5 Monster Zones[1].
  • Their battle position is always either face-up Attack Position or face-up Defense Position[1]. Monster Tokens cannot be in face-down Defense Position[2].
  • Monster Tokens cannot be sent to the Graveyard to activate effects[2].
  • Monster Tokens can be used as Synchro, Fusion ad Link Materials. In that case, they are not sent to the Graveyard and are removed from the Duel[2].
  • Tokens cannot be used as an Xyz Material[1].
  • Tokens on the field are treated as Normal Monsters[1].
  • If the activation or resolution of some effect is related to the Monster Token, those effects belong to the effect of the card that Special Summoned the Monster Token[2].
    Example The effect of an "Deus Token" summoned by the effect of "Darklord Asmodeus", which says ""Deus Token" cannot be destroyed by battle", is not applied as the effect of the "Deus Token", but rather as part of the effect of "Darklord Asmodeus".
    More on this point and effect negation:
    Example Question:
    If "Asmo Token" and "Deus Token" are Summoned by "Darklord Asmodeus"' effect, and "Skill Drain" is activated afterwards, can the "Asmo Token" be destroyed by card effects, and can the "Deus Token" be destroyed by battle?
    Answer:
    Tokens are treated as Normal Monsters, and have no effects.
    Therefore, even if "Skill Drain" begins applying after the Tokens are Special Summoned, the "Asmo Token" still cannot be destroyed by card effects, and the "Deus Token" still cannot be destroyed by battle. (Note that these tokens are indestructible due to "Darklord Asmodeus"' effect.)[5]
  • The original ATK of Monster Tokens is the value determined by the effect that summoned them. If an ATK/DEF value of "?" is printed on the effect that summoned the token, the original ATK/DEF of the token is "?"[3].
    Example Question:
    When my opponent's "Blue-Eyes White Dragon" attacks me directly and I take 3000 damage, I activate "The Golden Apples" and Special Summon a Malus Token.
    If my opponent then activates "Half Shut" and targets the Malus Token, what happens to its ATK and DEF?
    Answer:
    In the given situation, when you take 3000 battle damage and activate "The Golden Apples", the ATK and DEF of the Malus Token will each be 3000.
    When the effect of "Half Shut" is applied to the Malus Token, its current ATK of 3000 becomes half that value, so it will have 1500 ATK. (The DEF remains 3000.)
    Then, when the turn ends and the effect of "Half Shut" stops applying, the ATK of the Malus Token returns to its original value. (Since the original ATK of the Malus Token is "?", the ATK becomes 0. The ATK never returns to 3000, but the DEF remains 3000.)

    Banishing and tributing a Token is a conjunction success

    If a Token's (1) properties (Attribute, Type, ATK, DEF and/or Level...) satisfy a certain card's (2) activation requirement and (2) requires to banish a Monster with those properties, (2) can be activated. If (1) is banished because of (2)'s cost, (2) resolves normally even if (1) original properties were not originally the one necessary to fulfil the activation of (2)[1]. Examples:

    • Question:
      While the effect of "DNA Surgery" is applying, and Dragon-Type is declared, can a Monster Token treated as Dragon-Type be banished by the effect of "Dragon's Rebirth"?
      Answer:
      You can target a Monster Token treated as Dragon-Type to banish with the effect of "Dragon's Rebirth". Even in this case, the effect of Special Summoning a Dragon-Type monster from your hand or Graveyard is applied normally[1].
    • Question:
      I activate "Heavenly Dragon Circle" tributing a Tenyi Spirit Token. When resolving the effect, can I Special Summon a Wyrm Normal Monster from my Deck instead of adding it to my hand?
      Answer:
      As in the given situation, if you Tributed a non-Effect Monster to activate "Heavenly Dragon Circle", you can Special Summon a Wyrm Normal Monster from your Deck instead of adding it to your hand[2].
    • Question:
      Can I target a Token for the "target 1 face-up monster you control and this card; banish both those targets, then draw 2 cards" effect of "Cyber Valley"? If I do, will I still draw 2 cards?
      Answer:
      You can target a Token with the effect of "Cyber Valley" that draws 2 cards. When this effect then resolves, only "Cyber Valley" ends up banished, but you still draw 2 cards. (Note that the Monster Token disappeared upon leaving the field.)[3]
    • Question:
      Can a Fiend-Type Token be banished with the effect of "Archfiend Palabyrinth"?
      Answer:
      Yes, a Fiend-Type Token can be banished with the effect of "Archfiend Palabyrinth". Even in that case, the effect of Special Summoning an "Archfiend" monster still applies normally[4].
