If you have multiple monsters in your hand with Trigger Effects to Special Summon themselves (that activate in the hand in a particular timing), their effects cannot be activated in a chain and only 1 of them can be activated[1]. A "Trigger Effect that Special Summons the monster itself, and can be activated in the hand" also includes effects that can be activated in either the hand or the Graveyard, such as the effects of "Sacred Fire King Garunix" and "Dark Hole Dragon". Regardless of whether the effect is actually being activated in the hand or Graveyard, only one of this kind of Trigger Effect can be activated on any given Chain[3].

Example When you take battle damage while you have 2 copies of "Gorz the Emissary of Darkness", only the effect of 1 copy of "Gorz the Emissary of Darkness" that says "When you take damage from a card in your opponent's possession:" can be activated[1].

This also applies to the following monsters' Trigger Effects that Special Summon themselves, and can be activated from the hand; only one of these monsters can activate its relevant effect in any given Chain[3]:

  • Trigger Effects that require something to be "destroyed" or "sent to the Graveyard:" "Abominable Unchained Soul", "Legendary Fire King Ponix"
  • Trigger Effect that require something to be "banished:" "The Abyss Dragon Swordsoul"
  • Trigger Effect that can be activated "when a monster declares an attack:" "Spirit of Yubel", "Battle Fader"
  • Trigger Effect that have "damage" as a condition: "Tragoedia", "Gorz the Emissary of Darkness"
  • Trigger Effects that require a monster to be "Summoned:" "Rescue-ACE Fire Attacker", "Fantastical Dragon Phantazmay"
  • Trigger Effect that require something to be "Tributed:" "Darkest Diabolos, Lord of the Lair", "Primula the Rikka Fairy"
  • Trigger Effect that require something to be "activated:" "Altergeist Multifaker", "Magician of Dark Illusion"
  • Trigger Effects that require something being "added to the hand" or be "drawn:" "Snake-Eyes Poplar", "Trickstar Lilybell"
  • Trigger Effects that require "a monster to be flipped face-down:" "Subterror Behemoth Dragossuary", "Subterror Behemoth Stygokraken"
  • Trigger Effects that require you to "win a coin toss:" "Dark Sage"

Exception to this rule is when the hand summon triggers are mandatory effects: in which case they all activate and they form a chain[2].

Example Question:
Two copies of "Mist Valley Thunder Lord" activate their effects in the same Chain, each targeting a different copy of "Mist Valley Thunderbird" I control.
After this Chain resolves, will the Trigger effects of both copies of "Mist Valley Thunderbird" be activated?
Answer:
After the described Chain has resolved, the respective Trigger effects of each copy of "Mist Valley Thunderbird" will be activated as Chain Link 1 and Chain Link 2 respectively.
Afterwards, your opponent will have the first opportunity to activate Chain Link 3.
