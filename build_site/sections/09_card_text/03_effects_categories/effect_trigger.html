Trigger Effects

Effects that are activated in a particular written timing are Trigger Effects.

  • Trigger Effects start a chain when they activate.
  • Trigger Effects have a Spell Speed of 1. However, if multiple Trigger Effects activate at the same timing, they form a chain.
  • Since multiple effects that activate during a particular phase like the Standby Phase or End Phase are not treated as activating simultaneously, after activating and resolving 1 effect, other Trigger Effects can be activated and resolved.
  • In most cases, Trigger Effects do not activate if they meet their timing during the Damage Step. However, Trigger Effects that: activate mandatorily; activate when the monster itself is Special Summoned successfully; effects that activate when the monster is flipped face-up; and effects that activate when the monster is destroyed, banished, or added to the hand, can be activated.
  • Trigger Effects that activate when the monster is flipped face-up activate in most cases when that monster is changed from being face-down to face-up on the field. When monsters with Trigger Effects that activate when the monster is flipped face-up are attacked and flipped face-up, the effects activate during the timing of "after damage calculation" of that battle.
  • If a Trigger Effect is activated, and the monster that activated the effect leaves the field or is otherwise moved to a different location, the effect resolves afterwards. However, if something like "This card must be face-up on the field to activate and to resolve this effect." is written, so that this condition must be met at the time of the activation and at the time of the resolution, the effect is not applied.
References:
  1. OCG Perfect Rulebook 2017 pages 110-111

Activation Location

If a monster's Trigger Effect meets its activation conditions, but hasn't yet had a chance to actually activate yet (because it's still in the middle of a chain or card effect, for example) and is therefore being "saved for later", but its location (On the field, in the Graveyard, in the hand, banished, or in the Deck) changes between the time its Trigger Effect activation is met, and the time it actually has a chance to activate, its effect does not activate[1][3].

Examples:

  • Question:
    I have activated the effect of my "Drytron Alpha Thuban" by Tributing my "Cyber Angel Benten". As a result, "Cyber Angel Benten" was sent to the Graveyard.
    In response to the activation of "Drytron Alpha Thuban"'s effect, the effect of "D.D. Crow" was activated, targeting that "Cyber Angel Benten" in my Graveyard.
    After this chain resolves, can I activate the effect of the "Cyber Angel Benten" that was banished?
    Answer:
    In the given scenario, the effect of "Cyber Angel Benten" cannot be activated.
    If "Cyber Angel Benten" is Tributed, and sent to the Graveyard as a result, that effect can only be activated in the Graveyard. In other words, if, as in the situation given, "Cyber Angel Benten" is removed from the Graveyard after it was Tributed and sent there, but before the effect can be activated, that effect cannot be activated.
    On the other hand, if the Tributed "Cyber Angel Benten" is immediately banished as a result, such as due to the effect of "Macro Cosmos", the effect of the banished "Cyber Angel Benten" can be activated. In this case, if that "Cyber Angel Benten" is no longer banished by the time its effect can be activated - such as due to "Burial from a Different Dimension" - its effect cannot be activated[3].
  • Question:
    If a monster with one of the following effects is destroyed by "Terrifying Trap Hole Nightmare"'s effect, and then also banished by that same effect, can their respective effects be activated?
    1. "this card leaves the field" (e.g., the effect of "Mirrorjade the Iceblade Dragon")
    2. "this card is sent to the Graveyard" (e.g., the effect of "El Shaddoll Construct")
    3. "this card is destroyed" (e.g., the effect of "The Dark Magicians")
    Answer:
    None of the above effects can be activated.
    Regarding the 1. ("Mirrorjade the Iceblade Dragon") and 3. ("The Dark Magicians") options:
    If these cards are removed from the field and sent to the Graveyard, these effects can only be activated in the Graveyard, and the card must remain in the Graveyard to activate the effect. In the above situation, the monster is destroyed and sent to the Graveyard, then banished from the Graveyard. Therefore, the effects that activate "if this card leaves the field" or "if this card is destroyed" cannot be activated.
    Regarding the 2. ("El Shaddoll Construct") option:
    The effect of "El Shaddoll Construct" is an effect that activates in the Graveyard, in response to "El Shaddoll Construct" having been sent there. "El Shaddoll Construct" needs to be sent to the Graveyard, then remain there until this effect can be activated. Therefore, if "El Shaddoll Construct" is banished immediately after being sent to the Graveyard, its effect that activates "if this card is sent to the Graveyard" cannot be activated[4].

If, at the time of the particular timing, the card has been moved to a private location like the Deck or hand, the Trigger Effect does not activate[2].

Example If "Monster Reincarnation" is activated discarding "Dandylion" for the cost, and the same "Dandylion" is returned to the hand by the effect of that "Monster Reincarnation" that says "add it to your hand.", the effect of "Dandylion" that activates "If this card is sent to the Graveyard:" does not activate.

Multiple Hand Summon Monster Triggers

If you have multiple monsters in your hand with Trigger Effects to Special Summon themselves (that activate in the hand in a particular timing), their effects cannot be activated in a chain and only 1 of them can be activated[1].

Example When you take battle damage while you have 2 copies of "Gorz the Emissary of Darkness", only the effect of 1 copy of "Gorz the Emissary of Darkness" that says "When you take damage from a card in your opponent's possession:" can be activated.

Exception to this rule is when the hand summon triggers are mandatory effects: in which case they all activate and they form a chain[2].

Example Question:
Two copies of "Mist Valley Thunder Lord" activate their effects in the same Chain, each targeting a different copy of "Mist Valley Thunderbird" I control.
After this Chain resolves, will the Trigger effects of both copies of "Mist Valley Thunderbird" be activated?
Answer:
After the described Chain has resolved, the respective Trigger effects of each copy of "Mist Valley Thunderbird" will be activated as Chain Link 1 and Chain Link 2 respectively.
Afterwards, your opponent will have the first opportunity to activate Chain Link 3.

Changing card type prevents Trigger effects from activating

If, at the time of the particular timing, the card has changed card type like from monster becomes an equip card, the Trigger Effect does not activate.

Example Question:
When my "D/D/D Wave Oblivion King Caesar Ragnarok" declares an attack, its effect is activated, and my opponent activates "Call of the Haunted" in a chain by targeting "Black Rose Moonlight Dragon". When resolving the effect of "D/D/D Wave Oblivion King Caesar Ragnarok", if I choose to equip the Special Summoned "Black Rose Moonlight Dragon" to it, will that monster's effect be applied?
Answer:
In this scenario, you can still choose to equip the "Black Rose Moonlight Dragon" that was Special Summoned by "Call of the Haunted" using the effect of "D/D/D Wave Oblivion King Caesar Ragnarok". In that case, the Special Summoned "Black Rose Moonlight Dragon" becomes an Equip Card, and is no longer a monster, so after the current chain resolves, its effect cannot be activated. (In this case, after the chain resolves, since the number of monsters on your opponent's side of the field changed, a replay will occur.)

Changing phase and Trigger still activating

Trigger Effects still activate even when the phase changed during/after the resolution of an effect.

Example Question:
I activate the effect of "Cardcar D" by Tributing it, and that effect resolves. Afterwards, can I activate the effect of "Amorphage Infection"?
Answer:
In the situation you describe, "Cardcar D" is Tributed to activate its effect. When that effect resolves, it becomes the End Phase. Regardless, after "Cardcar D"'s effect resolves, the effect of "Amorphage Infection" can be activated.

Spell/Trap Cards will not "see" trigger before the card activation has resolved

Trigger effects of Spell/Trap Cards will not "see" their activation condition before the card activation has resolved[1]. Examples:

  • You activate "Union Hangar", but then chain "Call of the Haunted" and Special Summon a "Y-Dragon Head" from your Graveyard. In this scenario, although the LIGHT Machine-Type Union monster "Y-Dragon Head" was Special Summoned, since "Union Hangar" had not resolved on the field yet, you cannot activate its second effect afterward[1].
  • If you use "Metaverse" to activate "Labrynth Labyrinth", you cannot then activate "Labrynth Labyrinth"'s effect that Special Summons a Fiend monster [2]

Changing control and trigger effects

  • Monsters changing control after their Trigger effect meets its activation condition can activate their effects after they've changed control (in which case the player who took control of the monster activates it)[1].
    Example Question:
    I activate the effect of "Cyber Angel Natasha" in my Graveyard, banishing "Cyber Angel Benten" as a cost from my Graveyard and targeting my opponent's "Swordsoul Supreme Sovereign - Chengying". When resolving this effect, I gain control of "Swordsoul Supreme Sovereign - Chengying".
    In this case, can I activate the effect of "Swordsoul Supreme Sovereign - Chengying" to banish 1 card each from my opponent's field and Graveyard since "Cyber Angel Benten" has been banished?
    Answer:
    In the given situation, you can activate the effect of that "Swordsoul Supreme Sovereign - Chengying" you gained control of due the effect of "Cyber Angel Natasha".
  • You activate a card (1) that takes control of an opponent monster as Chain Link 1. (1) does not target. Your opponent activates as Chain Link 2 an effect that summons a monster (2). The chain resolves backwards, (2) is special summoned first, then with (1) you take control of an opponent's monster. If you take control of (2), after the chain has finished resolving, you are (2)'s controller. Therefore, if (2)'s has a Trigger effect on summon, you can activate its effect at this time[2].
    Example Question:
    I activate the "take control of 1 monster your opponent controls until the End Phase" effect of "Triple Tactics Talent", and my opponent activated the "immediately after this effect resolves, Link Summon 1 Link Monster using materials you control, including this card" effect of "I:P Masquerena" in response.
    As a result, "S:P Little Knight" is Link Summoned immediately after the effect of "I:P Masquerena" resolves, and I gain control of that "S:P Little Knight" when resolving the effect of "Triple Tactics Talent".
    In this case, which player can activate the "if this card is Link Summoned using a Fusion, Synchro, Xyz, or Link Monster as material" Trigger Effect of "S:P Little Knight"?
    Answer:
    After the chain has finished resolving (at the time when the Trigger Effect of "S:P Little Knight" can be activated), you are "S:P Little Knight"'s controller. Therefore, you can activate its effect at this time.

Monsters with a certain property being Summoned, and then losing that property before Trigger effects are checked for

Example: while "Harpies' Hunting Ground" is face-up on the field, Skill Drain is activated, and "Call of the Haunted" resolves in response, Special Summoning Harpie Queen. At the time that "Harpie Queen" is Special Summoned by the effect of "Call of the Haunted", its name is treated as "Harpie Lady". Afterwards, when the effect of Skill Drain applies, the effect of "Harpie Queen" is negated, and its name returns to being "Harpie Queen". Regardless, a "Harpie Lady" or "Harpie Lady Sisters" has been Special Summoned to the field, and the effect of "Harpies' Hunting Ground" must be activated.

Events are not responded to until the chain has resolved

Events are not responded to until the chain has resolved.

Example Question:
My opponent activates the effect of "Vision HERO Faris" that Special Summons it from the hand, by discarding a "Vision HERO Vyon" from their hand. In this situation, when can I activate "Consolation Prize"?
Answer:
If a monster is sent from the hand to the Graveyard as the cost of activating another card effect as in this scenario, "Consolation Prize" cannot be activated by chaining to that card effect. After that activated effect resolves, "Consolation Prize" can be activated in a new Chain.
Therefore, in this situation, when your opponent activates the second effect of "Vision HERO Faris" that places a "Vision HERO" monster from their Deck face-up in their Spell & Trap Zone, you can chain "Consolation Prize". (If your opponent does not activate the second effect of "Vision HERO Faris", you can activate "Consolation Prize" as Chain Link 1.)

Discarding for hand size for a second time, and Trigger effects

If you discard cards for hand size at the end of your End Phase, you can only activate effects on the Chain immediately following this. Even if, at some point after this first Chain, some other effect meets its activation condition, you cannot activate that effect.

Example Question:
At the end of my End Phase, I have 7 cards in my hand, and as a result, I discard "Danger! Nessie!". I activate and resolve "Danger! Nessie!"'s effect, adding "Danger! Bigfoot!" to my hand.
Since this results in my hand having 7 cards once again, I now discard "Danger! Bigfoot!". Can I activate "Danger! Bigfoot!"'s "if this card is discarded" effect?
Answer:
No, you cannot.
If you discard cards for hand size at the end of your End Phase, you can only activate effects on the Chain immediately following this. Even if, at some point after this first Chain, some other effect meets its activation condition, you cannot activate that effect.
