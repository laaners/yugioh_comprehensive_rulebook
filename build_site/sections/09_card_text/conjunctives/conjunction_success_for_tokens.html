Conjunction success for Tokens

If a Token's (1) properties (Attribute, Type, ATK, DEF and/or Level...) satisfy a certain card's (2) activation requirement and (2) requires to banish a Monster with those properties, (2) can be activated. If (1) is banished because of (2)'s cost, (2) resolves normally even if (1) original properties were not originally the one necessary to fulfil the activation of (2)[1]. Examples:

  • Question:
    While the effect of "DNA Surgery" is applying, and Dragon-Type is declared, can a Monster Token treated as Dragon-Type be banished by the effect of "Dragon's Rebirth"?
    Answer:
    You can target a Monster Token treated as Dragon-Type to banish with the effect of "Dragon's Rebirth". Even in this case, the effect of Special Summoning a Dragon-Type monster from your hand or Graveyard is applied normally[1].
  • Question:
    I activate "Heavenly Dragon Circle" tributing a Tenyi Spirit Token. When resolving the effect, can I Special Summon a Wyrm Normal Monster from my Deck instead of adding it to my hand?
    Answer:
    As in the given situation, if you Tributed a non-Effect Monster to activate "Heavenly Dragon Circle", you can Special Summon a Wyrm Normal Monster from your Deck instead of adding it to your hand[2].
  • Question:
    Can I target a Token for the "target 1 face-up monster you control and this card; banish both those targets, then draw 2 cards" effect of "Cyber Valley"? If I do, will I still draw 2 cards?
    Answer:
    You can target a Token with the effect of "Cyber Valley" that draws 2 cards. When this effect then resolves, only "Cyber Valley" ends up banished, but you still draw 2 cards. (Note that the Monster Token disappeared upon leaving the field.)[3]
  • Question:
    Can a Fiend-Type Token be banished with the effect of "Archfiend Palabyrinth"?
    Answer:
    Yes, a Fiend-Type Token can be banished with the effect of "Archfiend Palabyrinth". Even in that case, the effect of Special Summoning an "Archfiend" monster still applies normally[4].
