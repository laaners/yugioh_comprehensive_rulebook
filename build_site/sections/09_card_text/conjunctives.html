Conjunctives

The conjunctives "and", "and if you do", "also", and "then" have specific meanings[1]:

  • In "Do [A], then do [B]", [B] happens after [A]. These things happen in sequence, not simultaneously. [A] is required for [B], but NOT vice versa: if [A] does not happen, then stop; even if [B] cannot happen, you still do [A][1].
    Example Question:
    I activate "Magical Dimension", but "Vanity's Emptiness" is activated in response, so that no Special Summons can be performed. What happens when "Magical Dimension" resolves?
    Answer:
    In the proposed scenario, only the effect of "Magical Dimension" that tributes the targeted monster is applied.
    (No Spellcaster monster is Special Summoned from the hand, and no monster on the field can be destroyed.)[4]
  • In "Do [A] and [B]", both [A] and [B] happen simultaneously. BOTH [A] and [B] are required. If you cannot do both, you do nothing[1].
    Example "Number 53: Heart-eartH" says "When this card on the field is destroyed by a card effect while it has no Xyz Materials: You can Special Summon 1 "Number 92: Heart-eartH Dragon" from your Extra Deck and attach this card from the Graveyard to it as an Xyz Material." If "Number 53: Heart-eartH" was removed from the Graveyard with a Chained effect, you cannot Summon "Number 92: Heart-eartH Dragon".
  • In "Do [A], and if you do, do [B]", both [A] and [B] happen simultaneously. [A] is required for [B], but NOT vice versa: if [A] does not happen, then stop; even if [B] cannot happen, you still do [A][1].
  • In "Do [A], also do [B]", both [A] and [B] happen simultaneously. Neither is required for the other to occur[1].
  • In "Do [A], also, after that, do [B]", [B] happens after [A]. These things happen in sequence, not simultaneously. Neither is required for the other to occur (This conjunctive was never discussed in the Problem-Solving Card Text articles, as it was not introduced until 2014)[1].

ATTENTION: in all cases, even if [B] is not required or neither is required for the other, both must [A] and [B] must still meet the activation requirements of the card or effect in order to activate that card or effect[3].

Example Question:
If my opponent does not control any cards, can I activate the effect of "Madolche Queen Tiaramisu"? Also, when resolving the effect of "Madolche Queen Tiaramisu", if my opponent does not control any cards, how does the effect resolve?
Answer:
You cannot activate the effect of "Madolche Queen Tiaramisu" if your opponent controls no cards. When resolving the effect of "Madolche Queen Tiaramisu", if your opponent controls no cards, although you still return the targeted cards to your Deck, you do not apply the effect of returning your opponent's cards to the Deck.

There is no conjunctive that indicates both parts of the effect are required while also indicating that the parts are not simultaneous. While this relationship is rare, it does occur for some effects - in particular, Fusion Summoning and Ritual Summoning effects, which normally text structures unique to that Summoning method, which do not use conjunctives at all for that part of their text. The card "Luminous Dragon Ritual" uniquely indicates this relationship with the conjunctive "and" followed by "after that" at the end of the second part of the effect[2].
