Can only control 1 - Unique on the Field

Examples
Gravekeeper's Chief

You can only control 1 face-up "Gravekeeper's Chief". Your GY is unaffected by "Necrovalley". When this card is Tribute Summoned: You can target 1 "Gravekeeper's" monster in your GY; Special Summon that target.
Field Barrier

Field Spells on the field cannot be destroyed. Neither player can activate a new Field Spell Card. You can only control 1 "Field Barrier".
Imperial Custom

Face-up Continuous Traps cannot be destroyed by battle or card effect, except "Imperial Custom". You can only control 1 "Imperial Custom".
  • Monsters which violate this kind of effects are sent to the Graveyard and are not considered as destroyed by card effects[1].
    Example Question:
    Due to the effects of "DNA Surgery" and "Archfiend Zombie-Skull" that are face-up on the field, all monsters on the field become Zombie type and Zombie monsters you control cannot be destroyed by card effects. If the second "Gravekeeper's Chief" that has the effect that allows you to control only 1 face-up "Gravekeeper's Chief" is flipped face-up by an attack, will it be destroyed?
    Answer:
    When an effect that allows only 1 card of a specific name to be face-up on the field such as that of "Gravekeeper's Chief" is applied, if the second "Gravekeeper's Chief" is flipped face-up and thus destroyed, it is not treated as destroyed by a card effect.
    Therefore, even if the effect of "Archfiend Zombie-Skull" is applied, the second "Gravekeeper's Chief" that became a Zombie monster will be destroyed.
  • Monsters which violate this kind of effects when they flipped face-up by an attack, they will be destroyed after damage calculation is conducted[2].
    Example Question:
    If a "Malefic Blue-Eyes White Dragon", "Malefic Red-Eyes Black Dragon", "Malefic Stardust Dragon", etc., is already face-up on the field, and a face-down Defense Position "Malefic Blue-Eyes White Dragon" is flipped face-up by an attack, so that 2 "Malefic" monsters exist on the field, when will the flipped "Malefic Blue-Eyes White Dragon" be destroyed? Do you still perform damage calculation?
    Answer:
    If a face-up "Malefic Blue-Eyes White Dragon", etc., is already face-up on the field, and a face-down Defense Position "Malefic Blue-Eyes White Dragon" is flipped face-up by an attack, it will be destroyed by applying its own effect after damage calculation is conducted.
