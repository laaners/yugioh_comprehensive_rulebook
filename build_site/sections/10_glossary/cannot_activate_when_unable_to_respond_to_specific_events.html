Cannot activate when [event]: Unable to respond to when specific events happen

Examples
Gishki Vanity

During your Main Phase: You can discard this card; this turn, your opponent cannot activate Spells, Traps, Spell/Trap effects, or Monster Card effects when a "Gishki" monster is Ritual Summoned, or when a "Gishki" Ritual Spell Card is activated.
Branded Lost

The activation of your cards and effects that include an effect that Fusion Summons a Fusion Monster cannot be negated, also your opponent cannot activate cards or effects when a monster is Fusion Summoned this way. If you Fusion Summon a Fusion Monster: You can add 1 "Fallen of Albaz", or 1 monster that mentions it, from your Deck to your hand. You can only use this effect of "Branded Lost" once per turn.
Dust Storm of Gusto

Your opponent cannot activate cards or effects when "Gusto" monsters you control declare their attacks this turn.
  • These are cards that make so a player is unable to respond to when specific events happen[1].
    Example Question:
    While there is a "The Winged Dragon of Ra - Immortal Phoenix" in my Graveyard, both "The Winged Dragon of Ra" and "Fiendish Rhino Warrior" are simultaneously sent to the Graveyard due to the effect of "Dark Hole".
    In this situation, how are the effect of "The Winged Dragon of Ra - Immortal Phoenix" that summons it from the Graveyard and the effect of "Fiendish Rhino Warrior" that sends a Fiend monster to the Graveyard processed?
    Answer:
    While "The Winged Dragon of Ra - Immortal Phoenix" is in the Graveyard, its effect that summons it from the Graveyard must always be activated if "The Winged Dragon of Ra" is sent from the field to the Graveyard. Furthermore, neither player can activate cards or effects in response to that effect's activation.
    Therefore, in the given situation, the effect of "The Winged Dragon of Ra - Immortal Phoenix" must be activated as Chain Link 1, and will then resolve.
    (As it is not possible to activate cards or effects in response to this activation, the effect of the "Fiendish Rhino Warrior" that was sent to the Graveyard cannot be activated.)[4]
  • If an effect of this kind is applying that makes so only the opponent cannot respond to a specific event (1), but you chain another card (2) when (1) happens, the opponent is now able to respond to (1)[2][3].
    Example Question:
    I control "Magical Meltdown" or "Branded Lost", and the respective "your opponent cannot activate cards or effects when a monster is Fusion Summoned" effect is applying.
    During my turn, my opponent activates "Tri-Brigade Revolt" as Chain Link 1, and I activate the effect of "Shaddoll Schism" as Chain Link 2 in response. When this Chain resolves, I first Fusion Summon "El Shaddoll Construct", and then my opponent Link Summons "Tri-Brigade Shuraig the Ominous Omen".
    In this situation, can my opponent then activate the effect of "Tri-Brigade Shuraig the Ominous Omen" after I activate the effect of my "El Shaddoll Construct"?
    Answer:
    Yes, they can.
    The activation of Chain Link 1 resolves strictly after you have Fusion Summoned "El Shaddoll Construct". Therefore, after the Chain finishes resolving, it is not "when a monster is Fusion Summoned this way".
