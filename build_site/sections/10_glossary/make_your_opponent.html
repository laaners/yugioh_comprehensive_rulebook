Make your opponent/Your opponent must do [A]

Examples
Apoqliphort Towers

Cannot be Special Summoned. Requires 3 "Qli" Tributes to Normal Summon/Set. If this card is Normal Summoned/Set, it is unaffected by Spell/Trap effects and by activated effects from any monster whose original Level/Rank is lower than this card's current Level. All Special Summoned monsters lose 500 ATK and DEF. Once per turn: You can make your opponent send 1 monster from their hand or their side of the field to the Graveyard (their choice).
Herald of the Abyss

Pay 1500 LP and declare 1 Monster Type and 1 Attribute; your opponent must send 1 face-up monster with the declared Type and Attribute from their field to the GY, if possible. For the rest of this turn, your opponent cannot activate the effects of monsters with that sent monster's name. You can only activate 1 "Herald of the Abyss" per turn.
There Can Be Only One

Each player can only control 1 monster of each Type. If a player controls 2 or more monsters of the same Type, they must send some to the GY so they control no more than 1 monster of that Type.
  • Your opponent is the one who does the listed procedure in the card text[1].
    Example Question:
    While the effect of "Chaos Hunter" is applying, can I activate "Evenly Matched"?
    Answer:
    When resolving your "Evenly Matched", your opponent is the one who banishes their cards, so if you have "Chaos Hunter" on your field, you cannot activate "Evenly Matched".
    (Conversely, if your opponent has "Chaos Hunter" on their field, you can still activate "Evenly Matched".)
  • This is not a card effect that affects monsters but it's an effect applied to your opponent[2]. Examples:
    • Question:
      When my opponent's "Evenly Matched" resolves, there are multiple cards on my field, including a "Metaphys Executor". What happens?
      Answer:
      You still follow "Evenly Matched"'s text. (You can also choose "Metaphys Executor" as the monster to be banished.)
      The effect of "Evenly Matched" is an effect that is applied to the activating player's opponent. When this effect is applied, that opponent will banish cards on their field, face-down. In such a case, these cards are not treated as being banished by a card effect.
      Therefore, when activating or resolving "Evenly Matched", the first effect of "Metaphys Executor", which makes it unable to be banished by card effects, is irrelevant.
      This also applies to the following cards' effects that prevent banishing by card effect, in place of "Metaphys Executor": "Libromancer Firestarter", and "Libromancer Bonded"[2].
    • Question:
      I control the Machine-Type "Ancient Gear Howitzer", and the Warrior-Type "Number 86: Heroic Champion - Rhongomyniad". Both are applying their respective "unaffected by other cards' effects" effects.
      My opponent's "Evenly Matched" now resolves while it is the only card on their field. Do I need to banish one of "Ancient Gear Howitzer" and "Number 86: Heroic Champion - Rhongomyniad" face-down?
      Answer:
      You must choose one of them, and banish it face-down.
      When "Evenly Matched" resolves, its effect is applied to your opponent. As a result, your opponent must choose cards on their field, and banish them face-down. These cards, which are banished during the resolution of "Evenly Matched", are not treated as being banished by a card effect.
      Therefore, even monsters that are unaffected by Trap effects will be banished face-down. Additionally, these monsters are not treated as having left the field due to a Trap effect[3].
  • Cards sent to the Graveyard by this procedure are not considered to be sent to the Graveyard by a card effect[4].
    Example Question:
    The opponent sent a "Shaddoll Falco" to the Graveyard as a result of the effect of "Apoqliphort Towers" that makes the opponent send a monster to the Graveyard.
    In this case, can the effect of "Shaddoll Falco" that Special Summons itself be activated?
    Answer:
    If the effect of "Apoqliphort Towers" is applied, it makes the opponent send a monster they control or in their hand to the Graveyard.
    At that time, even if the opponent sent "Shaddoll Falco" to the Graveyard, that "Shaddoll Falco" is not considered to be sent to the Graveyard by a card effect.
    Therefore, the effect of "Shaddoll Falco" that Special Summons itself cannot be activated.
