Summon from Spell/Trap/Pendulum Zone

Examples
Centur-Ion Primera

While this card is a Continuous Trap, Level 5 or higher "Centur-Ion" monsters you control cannot be destroyed by card effects. You can only use each of the following effects of "Centur-Ion Primera" once per turn. If this card is Normal or Special Summoned: You can add 1 "Centur-Ion" card from your Deck to your hand, except "Centur-Ion Primera", also you cannot Special Summon "Centur-Ion Primera" for the rest of this turn. During the Main Phase, if this card is a Continuous Trap: You can Special Summon this card.
Divine Temple of the Snake-Eye

When this card is activated: You can place 1 "Snake-Eye" monster from your hand, Deck, or GY face-up in its owner's Spell & Trap Zone as a Continuous Spell. Level 1 FIRE monsters you control gain 1100 ATK. Once per turn, if your opponent Normal or Special Summons a monster(s) (except during the Damage Step): You can target 1 Monster Card on the field treated as a Continuous Spell; Special Summon it to your field. You can only activate 1 "Divine Temple of the Snake-Eye" per turn.
Pendulum Switch

You can target 1 card in your Pendulum Zone; Special Summon it. You can target 1 Pendulum Monster in your Monster Zone; place that Pendulum Monster in your Pendulum Zone. You can only use 1 "Pendulum Switch" effect per turn, and only once that turn.
  • An effect (1) targets a Monster Card (2) in the Spell/Trap Zone ((1) is not treated as a Monster). If another card is chained to (1) and summons (2) to the Monster Zone, then (2) is no longer treated as the same card that was targeted by (1), so (1) does not resolve successfully[1][4].
    Example: while a Centur-Ion Primera is treated as a Continuous Trap in the Spell & Trap Zone, "Baronne de Fleur"'s "target 1 card on the field; destroy it" effect has been activated by targeting it. In response, Centur-Ion Primera's effect is activated, which Special Summons it from the Spell & Trap Zone and it resolves. By the time "Baronne de Fleur"'s effect resolves, the Centur-Ion Primera has changed from being a Continuous Trap to being a Monster. (It is no longer a Continuous Trap.) As a result, it is no longer targeted by the effect of "Baronne de Fleur" and nothing happens.
  • Similary, if (1) targets (2) while in a Monster Zone but (2) is placed in the Spell/Trap Zone, (2) is no longer treated as the same card that was targeted by (1), so (1) does not resolve successfully[2].
  • An effect (1) targets a Spell/Trap Card (2) in the Spell/Trap Zone. If another card is chained to (1) and summons (2) to the Monster Zone (and (2) is not treated as a Spell/Trap at resolution), then (2) is no longer treated as the same card that was targeted by (1), so (1) does not resolve successfully[3].
    Example: my opponent has activated "Raigeki Break" by targeting a Set The "Phantom Knights of Shade Brigandine" in my Spell & Trap Zone. If I now activate that"Phantom Knights of Shade Brigandine" in response, "Phantom Knights of Shade Brigandine" will first be Special Summoned when resolving its Trap Card. At this time, since it is no longer treated as a Trap Card, it also stops being targeted by "Raigeki Break". Therefore, "Raigeki Break"'s effect is then not applied.
  • Changing from only-Trap to Monster Trap, it does not create a new copy[5].
    Example: your opponent has activated "Raigeki Break" by targeting a Set "Conquistador of the Golden Land" in your Spell & Trap Zone. If you now activate that "Conquistador of the Golden Land" in response, you first Special Summon it as a Normal Monster (which is still also treated as a Trap) to the Monster Zone, and potentially destroying a card on the field. Then, after that, that "Conquistador of the Golden Land" in the Monster Zone will be destroyed when the effect of "Raigeki Break" resolves.

Monster Tokens

Tokens cannot be Special Summoned from the Spell & Trap Zone[1].

Example "The Grand Jupiter" can be equipped with a Token by its own effect. The equipped Token cannot be targeted when activating the last effect of "The Grand Jupiter" that Special Summons one of the monsters equipped to it[1]. Similarly, "Divine Temple of the Snake-Eye" can't summon a token from the Spell/Trap Zone[2].
