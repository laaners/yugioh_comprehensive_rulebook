Summon from Spell/Trap/Pendulum Zone

Examples
Centur-Ion Primera

While this card is a Continuous Trap, Level 5 or higher "Centur-Ion" monsters you control cannot be destroyed by card effects. You can only use each of the following effects of "Centur-Ion Primera" once per turn. If this card is Normal or Special Summoned: You can add 1 "Centur-Ion" card from your Deck to your hand, except "Centur-Ion Primera", also you cannot Special Summon "Centur-Ion Primera" for the rest of this turn. During the Main Phase, if this card is a Continuous Trap: You can Special Summon this card.
Divine Temple of the Snake-Eye

When this card is activated: You can place 1 "Snake-Eye" monster from your hand, Deck, or GY face-up in its owner's Spell & Trap Zone as a Continuous Spell. Level 1 FIRE monsters you control gain 1100 ATK. Once per turn, if your opponent Normal or Special Summons a monster(s) (except during the Damage Step): You can target 1 Monster Card on the field treated as a Continuous Spell; Special Summon it to your field. You can only activate 1 "Divine Temple of the Snake-Eye" per turn.
Pendulum Switch

You can target 1 card in your Pendulum Zone; Special Summon it. You can target 1 Pendulum Monster in your Monster Zone; place that Pendulum Monster in your Pendulum Zone. You can only use 1 "Pendulum Switch" effect per turn, and only once that turn.
  • Regarding Monster cards:
    • Changing from only-Spell/Trap to only-Monster creates a new copy: an effect (1) targets a Monster Card (2) in the Spell/Trap Zone ((1) is not treated as a Monster). If another card is chained to (1) and summons (2) to the Monster Zone, then (2) is no longer treated as the same card that was targeted by (1), so (1) does not resolve successfully[1][4].
      Example Question:
      While a "Centur-Ion Primera" is treated as a Continuous Trap in the Spell & Trap Zone, "Baronne de Fleur"'s "target 1 card on the field; destroy it" effect has been activated by targeting it. In response, "Centur-Ion Primera"'s third effect, which Special Summons it from the Spell & Trap Zone, resolves.
      As a result, "Centur-Ion Primera" has been Special Summoned to the Monster Zone by the time "Baronne de Fleur"'s effect resolves. What happens to that effect?
      Answer:
      When the effect resolves, nothing happens.
      Due to its third effect, the "Centur-Ion Primera" has changed from being a Continuous Trap to being a Monster. (It is no longer a Continuous Trap.) As a result, it is no longer targeted by the effect of "Baronne de Fleur".
    • Changing from only-Monster to only-Spell/Trap creates a new copy: similary, if (1) targets (2) while in a Monster Zone but (2) is placed in the Spell/Trap Zone, (2) is no longer treated as the same card that was targeted by (1), so (1) does not resolve successfully[2].
      Example Question:
      "Stargazer Magician" is face-up in my Monster Zone, and "Pendulum Switch" is face-up in my Spell & Trap Zone. In this situation, if my opponent activates "Raigeki Break" and targets "Stargazer Magician", but I chain the effect of "Pendulum Switch" that places it in the Pendulum Zone, what happens?
      Answer:
      In this scenario, when "Raigeki Break" resolves, the targeted "Stargazer Magician" will be in your Pendulum Zone due to the effect of "Pendulum Switch". In this case, "Stargazer Magician" is no longer treated as the same card that was targeted by "Raigeki Break", so it is not destroyed.
  • Regarding Spell/Trap cards:
    • Changing from only-Spell/Trap to only-Monster creates a new copy: an effect (1) targets a Spell/Trap Card (2) in the Spell/Trap Zone. If another card is chained to (1) and summons (2) to the Monster Zone (and (2) is not treated as a Spell/Trap at resolution), then (2) is no longer treated as the same card that was targeted by (1), so (1) does not resolve successfully[3].
      Example Question:
      My opponent has activated "Raigeki Break" by targeting a Set "The Phantom Knights of Shade Brigandine" in my Spell & Trap Zone. If I now activate that "The Phantom Knights of Shade Brigandine" in response, what happens when this Chain resolves?
      Answer:
      In the situation you describe, "The Phantom Knights of Shade Brigandine" will first be Special Summoned when resolving its Trap Card activation and applying its first effect. At this time, since it is no longer treated as a Trap Card, it also stops being targeted by "Raigeki Break". Therefore, "Raigeki Break"'s effect is then not applied.
      This also applies to the following Trap Cards, which also have effects that Special Summon them as a monster (without being treated as a Trap Card): "Traptrix Holeutea", "The Phantom Knights of Wrong Magnetring", "The Phantom Knights of Lost Vambrace", "Qlife's End", and "The Phantom Knights of Tomb Shield".
    • Changing from only-Spell/Trap to Monster Trap, it does not create a new copy[5].
      Example Question:
      My opponent has activated "Raigeki Break" by targeting a Set "Conquistador of the Golden Land" in my Spell & Trap Zone. If I now activate that "Conquistador of the Golden Land" in response, what happens?
      Answer:
      When this Chain resolves, you first resolve "Conquistador of the Golden Land"'s first effect, Special Summoning it as a Normal Monster (which is still also treated as a Trap) to the Monster Zone, and potentially destroying a card on the field.
      Then, after that, that Conquistador of the Golden Lord in the Monster Zone will be destroyed when the effect of "Raigeki Break" resolves. (Since "Conquistador of the Golden Land" is still treated as a Trap after being Special Summoned, it will still be targeted by "Raigeki Break" even after it is Special Summoned.)
