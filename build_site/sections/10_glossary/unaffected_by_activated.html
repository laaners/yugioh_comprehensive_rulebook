Unaffected by activated effects

Examples
Expurrely Noir

2 Level 7 monsters
You can also Xyz Summon this card by using a Rank 2 monster you control with 5 or more materials. (Transfer its materials to this card.) This card is unaffected by your opponent's activated effects while it has 5 or more materials. You can detach 2 materials from this card, then target 1 card your opponent controls or in their GY; place it on the bottom of the Deck. This is a Quick Effect if this card has a Level 1 "Purrely" monster as material.

Sangen Summoning

During your Main Phase 1, FIRE Dragon monsters you control are unaffected by your opponent's activated effects. During your Main Phase: You can add 1 "Tenpai Dragon" monster from your Deck to your hand, then discard 1 card. You can only use this effect of "Sangen Summoning" once per turn. If this card is destroyed during the Battle Phase: You can target 1 Dragon Synchro Monster you control; double its ATK.
Ancient Gear Duel

"Ancient Gear Golem", and monsters that mention it, in your Monster Zone are unaffected by your opponent's activated monster effects. If your opponent controls a monster: You can Fusion Summon 1 Fusion Monster that mentions "Ancient Gear Golem" from your Extra Deck, by banishing monsters from your field or GY as material, including "Ancient Gear Golem" you control, and if you do, it can make up to 3 attacks during each Battle Phase. You can only use this effect of "Ancient Gear Duel" once per turn.
  • Monsters which are "unaffected by activated effects" will be unaffected by any effect that is applied when the Chain Link formed by an activation resolves. However, any other effect will apply to them normally[1].
  • Continuous/lingering effects that begin applying when the Chain Link formed by the card activation resolves, these effects will continue to apply to monsters afterwards. Therefore, these effects can affect monsters that are "unaffected by activated effects"[1].
    Example The effects of "Skill Drain" and "Dimensional Barrier" will negate the effects of "Cyberdark End Dragon", and the effect of "Crackdown" will take control of "Cyberdark End Dragon".
  • Effects that apply at a later point can affect monsters that are "unaffected by activated effects"[2].
    Example Your opponent controls a "Cyberdark End Dragon", and its effect that makes it unaffected by your activated effects is applying. You control a "Blue-Eyes White Dragon", to which the effect of "The Ultimate Creature of Destruction" has been applied, causing any monster it battles to be destroyed at the End of the Damage Step. If the two monsters battle, you receive 2000 points of Battle Damage when damage calculation is conducted, which is the difference between the monsters' ATK. Then, at the End of the Damage Step, the opponent's "Cyberdark End Dragon" is destroyed. Here, the effect of "The Ultimate Creature of Destruction" that destroys an opponent's monster that battles your targeted monster is not an effect that applies while resolving the Chain Link formed by "The Ultimate Creature of Destruction"'s activation. Instead, it is an effect that applies later, at the End of the Damage Step, when the monster that was targeted by the effect battles. Therefore, it can destroy a monster that is unaffected by "The Ultimate Creature of Destruction"'s activated effect.
