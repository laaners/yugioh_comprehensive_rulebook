Traps Treated as Monsters (not treated as Traps)

Examples
The Phantom Knights of Shade Brigandine

Special Summon this card in Defense Position as a Normal Monster (Warrior/DARK/Level 4/ATK 0/DEF 300). (This card is NOT treated as a Trap.) If you have no Traps in your GY, you can activate this card the turn it was Set. You can only activate 1 "The Phantom Knights of Shade Brigandine" per turn.
The Prime Monarch

Once per turn: You can target 2 "Monarch" Spells/Traps in your GY; shuffle them into the Deck, then draw 1 card. If this card is in your GY: You can banish 1 other "Monarch" Spell/Trap from your GY; Special Summon this card in Defense Position as a Normal Monster (Fairy/LIGHT/Level 5/ATK 1000/DEF 2400). (This card is NOT treated as a Trap.) You can only use this effect of "The Prime Monarch" once per turn.
Paleozoic Dinomischus

Target 1 face-up card on the field; discard 1 card, and if you do, banish it. Once per Chain, when a Trap Card is activated while this card is in your GY: You can Special Summon this card as a Normal Monster (Aqua/WATER/Level 2/ATK 1200/DEF 0). (This card is NOT treated as a Trap.) If Summoned this way, this card is unaffected by monster effects, also banish it when it leaves the field.
  • They are "Trap Cards" but they are not "Traps"[4].
    Example Question:
    In my Monster Zone, there are a "The First Monarch" and a "The Prime Monarch", both of which were Special Summoned by their respective effects. "The First Monarch" is also treated as a Trap, while "The Prime Monarch" is not treated as a Trap.
    In this situation, I resolve "Apophis the Swamp Deity", and its effect allows me to negate cards my opponent controls, up to the number of other Continuous Traps I control. How many cards can I negate?
    Answer:
    In the situation described, you can only negate the effects of one card your opponent controls.
    The "The First Monarch", which was Special Summoned by its own effect, is treated both as a monster, and as a Continuous Trap Card, while in the Monster Zone.
    On the other hand, the "The Prime Monarch", which was Special Summoned by its own effect, is treated as a monster while in the Monster Zone, but is not treated as a Continuous Trap Card.
  • The original ATK of Traps treated as monsters is the value determined by the effect that summoned them[2].
  • An effect (1) targets a Spell/Trap Card (2) in the Spell/Trap Zone. If another card is chained to (1) and summons (2) to the Monster Zone (and (2) is not treated as a Spell/Trap at resolution), then (2) is no longer treated as the same card that was targeted by (1), so (1) does not resolve successfully[3].
    Example Question:
    My opponent has activated "Raigeki Break" by targeting a Set "The Phantom Knights of Shade Brigandine" in my Spell & Trap Zone. If I now activate that "The Phantom Knights of Shade Brigandine" in response, what happens when this Chain resolves?
    Answer:
    In the situation you describe, "The Phantom Knights of Shade Brigandine" will first be Special Summoned when resolving its Trap Card activation and applying its first effect. At this time, since it is no longer treated as a Trap Card, it also stops being targeted by "Raigeki Break". Therefore, "Raigeki Break"'s effect is then not applied.
    This also applies to the following Trap Cards, which also have effects that Special Summon them as a monster (without being treated as a Trap Card): "Traptrix Holeutea", "The Phantom Knights of Wrong Magnetring", "The Phantom Knights of Lost Vambrace", "Qlife's End", and "The Phantom Knights of Tomb Shield".
