Properties Changing - Copy effect resolves as another card's effect

Examples
Dinomorphia Kentregina

2 "Dinomorphia" monsters with different names
Loses ATK equal to your LP. You can only use each of the following effects of "Dinomorphia Kentregina" once per turn. During the Main Phase (Quick Effect): You can pay half your LP and banish 1 "Dinomorphia" Normal Trap from your GY; this effect becomes that Trap's effect when that card is activated. If this card is destroyed by battle or card effect: You can Special Summon 1 Level 4 or lower "Dinomorphia" monster from your GY.

Spellbook of the Master

If you control a Spellcaster-Type monster: You can reveal 1 other "Spellbook" card in your hand, then target 1 "Spellbook" Normal Spell Card in your Graveyard, except "Spellbook of the Master"; this card's effect becomes that target's effect. You can only activate 1 "Spellbook of the Master" per turn.
Transaction Rollback

Pay half your LP, then target 1 Normal Trap in your opponent's GY, except "Transaction Rollback"; this effect becomes that card's activation effect. You can banish this card from your GY and pay half your LP, then target 1 Normal Trap in your GY, except "Transaction Rollback"; this effect becomes that card's activation effect. You can only use 1 "Transaction Rollback" effect per turn, and only once that turn.
  • If you activate a card that copies a card's effect [X][4]:
    • You can disregard any "once per turn" restrictions.
    • You can ignore costs.
    • However, activation conditions of [X] must still be met
    If you activate an effect that copies a card's effect [X][4]:
    • You can disregard any "once per turn" restrictions.
    • You can ignore costs.
    • You can ignore any activation conditions and other restrictions that only apply when activating [X], since you are not activating the a card that copies but an effect that copies.
    • However, you still need to meet any activation conditions for activating [X]'s effect
    If any parts of [X] require [X] to have been activated, such as if "For the rest of this turn after this card resolves, (...)" is specified, those parts are only applied if you activated a card that copies, but are not applied if you activated an effect that copies.
    Example Question:
    This question is regarding the first and second effects of "Transaction Rollback", both of which become a targeted Trap Card's effect when they resolve.
    Ⓐ: According to the text of "Armory Call", "You can only activate 1 ""Armory Call"" per turn." If I have previously activated "Armory Call" this turn, can I activate "Transaction Rollback" by targeting "Armory Call"?
    Ⓑ: If I activate "Transaction Rollback" by targeting "Heavy Storm Duster", what happens to the "You cannot conduct your Battle Phase the turn you activate this card." limitation imposed by "Heavy Storm Duster"'s text?
    Ⓒ: If I activate "Transaction Rollback" by targeting "Giant Starfall", does "Neither player can activate the effects of monsters without a Level in response to this card's activation." apply?
    Ⓓ: If it is not currently the End Step of the Battle Phase, can I activate "Transaction Rollback" by targeting "Evenly Matched", which has the "At the end of the Battle Phase, (...)" activation condition?
    Ⓔ: If I activate "Transaction Rollback" by targeting "Eldlixir of Scarlet Sanguine", does the "For the rest of this turn after this card resolves, you cannot Special Summon monsters, except Zombie monsters." effect apply?
    Answer:
    With regard to "Transaction Rollback"'s first effect, you can disregard any "once per turn" restrictions listed on the original Normal Trap being targeted. Additionally, if that Normal Trap requires a cost to be paid, that cost can also be ignored. However, any activation conditions of the Normal Trap being targeted must still be met. (This also applies to "Trap of Darkness", "Fake Feather", and "Labrynth Barrage".)
    Similarly, with regard to "Transaction Rollback"'s second effect, you can also disregard any "once per turn" restrictions, and any costs, listed on the original Normal Trap being targeted. Additionally, since activating "Transaction Rollback"'s second effect is not activating a Trap Card, you can ignore any activation conditions and other restrictions that only apply when activating a Trap Card. However, you still need to meet any activation conditions for activating that card's effect. (This also applies to "Junk Collector"'s effect.)
    Finally, when resolving the first or second effect of "Transaction Rollback", generally speaking, the entire effect that is listed as the effect of resolving the targeted card's Card activation is applied. However, if any parts of the effect require a Trap Card to have been activated, such as if "For the rest of this turn after this card resolves, (...)" is specified, those parts are only applied if "Transaction Rollback" itself was activated (i.e., its first effect is resolving), but are not applied if this is not the case (i.e., "Transaction Rollback"'s second effect is resolving).
    Ⓐ: You can activate either of "Transaction Rollback"'s effects by targeting "Armory Call", even if "Armory Call" was previously activated this turn. As mentioned previously, you can disregard the "You can only activate 1 ""Armory Call"" per turn." text.
    Ⓑ: The "You cannot conduct your Battle Phase the turn you activate this card." text is a limitation on "Heavy Storm Duster"'s Trap Card activation. Therefore, you need to follow it if "Heavy Storm Duster" is targeted by "Transaction Rollback"'s first effect. (You cannot target "Heavy Storm Duster" if the Battle Phase was conducted this turn, and cannot conduct the Battle Phase if "Heavy Storm Duster" was previously targeted.) However, you can ignore this limitation if targeting "Heavy Storm Duster" for "Transaction Rollback"'s second effect.
    Ⓒ: The "Neither player can activate the effects of monsters without a Level in response to this card's activation." text of "Giant Starfall" is neither an activation requirement, nor part of its effect's resolution. Therefore, it is not considered when "Giant Starfall" is targeted by the first or second effect of "Transaction Rollback". (Players are allowed to activate the effects of monsters without a Level in response to the activation of "Transaction Rollback", or its effect.)
    Ⓓ: You cannot activate "Transaction Rollback", or its effect, by targeting "Evenly Matched" in the situation given. Both "at the end of the Battle Phase" and "if your opponent controls more cards than you do" are activation conditions for activating "Evenly Matched"'s effect. Therefore, both requirements must be met if either "Transaction Rollback", or its effect, is activated by targeting "Evenly Matched".
    Ⓔ: The "For the rest of this turn after this card resolves, you cannot Special Summon monsters, except Zombie monsters." effect is applied as part of the effect applied during the resolution of "Eldlixir of Scarlet Sanguine"'s Card activation. However, it only applies if a Card was activated. Therefore, it will only be applied if "Transaction Rollback" was activated (and its first effect is now resolving), but will not be applied if only "Transaction Rollback"'s (second) effect was activated (and is now resolving).
  • Texts that are not effects are not copied[4].
  • If a card (1) says "do [A] at/until [X] turn after this card's activation" ("keeps track" of when (1) was activated), (1)'s effect cannot be copied by activating an effect that copies effects such as "Transaction Rollback"'s Graveyard effect. However, it can be copied by activating a card that copies effects such as by activating a set "Transaction Rollback"[3][4].
    Example Question:
    Can I activate "Trap of Darkness", "Fake Feather" or "Transaction Rollback" by targeting "Extinction on Schedule", or activate "Labrynth Barrage" in response to "Extinction on Schedule"'s activation?
    Furthermore, can I activate "Transaction Rollback"'s effect by targeting "Extinction on Schedule", or banish "Extinction on Schedule" to activate "Junk Collector"'s effect?
    Answer:
    If it is currently either player's Main Phase, you can activate "Trap of Darkness", "Fake Feather" or "Transaction Rollback" by targeting "Extinction on Schedule".
    If you do, at the end of the third Battle Phase after the respective card was activated, each player must send all cards on their field to the Graveyard normally.
    However, you cannot activate "Transaction Rollback"'s effect by targeting "Extinction on Schedule", and cannot activate "Junk Collector"'s effect by banishing "Extinction on Schedule".
  • You activate a card (1) that says "You can only apply this effect of [card name] once per Duel". As Chain Link 2 you activate a card (2) that copies the effect of (1). The chain resolves backwards, (2) resolves as (1)'s effect and (1) resolves without applying its effect since you can only apply (1)'s effect once per Duel[1].
    Example Question:
    Can "Serial Spell" be activated in a Chain to the activation of "Rank-Up-Magic - The Seventh One"?
    Answer:
    "Serial Spell" can be activated in a Chain to the activation of "Rank-Up-Magic - The Seventh One".
    In that case, when "Serial Spell" resolves, you will Special Summon 1 monster that has a number between "Number 101"~"Number 107" in its name, except a "Number C" monster, then Special Summon 1 "Number C" monster with the same number by overlaying it on the first monster and using it as material.
    After that, "Rank-Up-Magic - The Seventh One" resolves without Special Summoning any monsters. (You can only perform the Special Summons from the effect of "Rank-Up-Magic - The Seventh One" once per Duel.)
  • A card (1) copies the effect of another card (2) and applies the effects of (2) at resolution: the applied effects it's still treated as applying (1)'s name effect[2].
    Example Question:
    If I activate "Joker's Wild" by sending "Royal Straight" to the Graveyard, can I then Special Summon "Royal Straight Slasher"?
    Answer:
    No, you cannot.
    When "Joker's Wild" resolves, the sent Spell's effect is applied as the effect of "Joker's Wild". Therefore, the Special Summoned monster would be considered to have been Special Summoned by "Joker's Wild", not by "Royal Straight". As a result, "Royal Straight Slasher", which must be Special Summoned by "Royal Straight", cannot be Special Summoned.
