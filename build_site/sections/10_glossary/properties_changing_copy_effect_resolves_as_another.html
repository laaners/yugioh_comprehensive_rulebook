Properties Changing: Copy effect resolves as another card's effect

Examples
Dinomorphia Kentregina

2 "Dinomorphia" monsters with different names
Loses ATK equal to your LP. You can only use each of the following effects of "Dinomorphia Kentregina" once per turn. During the Main Phase (Quick Effect): You can pay half your LP and banish 1 "Dinomorphia" Normal Trap from your GY; this effect becomes that Trap's effect when that card is activated. If this card is destroyed by battle or card effect: You can Special Summon 1 Level 4 or lower "Dinomorphia" monster from your GY.

Spellbook of the Master

If you control a Spellcaster-Type monster: You can reveal 1 other "Spellbook" card in your hand, then target 1 "Spellbook" Normal Spell Card in your Graveyard, except "Spellbook of the Master"; this card's effect becomes that target's effect. You can only activate 1 "Spellbook of the Master" per turn.
Transaction Rollback

Pay half your LP, then target 1 Normal Trap in your opponent's GY, except "Transaction Rollback"; this effect becomes that card's activation effect. You can banish this card from your GY and pay half your LP, then target 1 Normal Trap in your GY, except "Transaction Rollback"; this effect becomes that card's activation effect. You can only use 1 "Transaction Rollback" effect per turn, and only once that turn.
  • You activate a card (1) that says "You can only apply this effect of [card name] once per Duel". As Chain Link 2 you activate a card (2) that copies the effect of (1). The chain resolves backwards, (2) resolves as (1)'s effect and (1) resolves without applying its effect since you can only apply (1)'s effect once per Duel[1].
    Example "Serial Spell" can be activated in a Chain to the activation of "Rank-Up-Magic - The Seventh One". In that case, when "Serial Spell" resolves, you will Special Summon 1 monster that has a number between "Number 101" and "Number 107" in its name, except a "Number C" monster, then Special Summon 1 "Number C" monster with the same number by overlaying it on the first monster and using it as material. After that, "Rank-Up-Magic - The Seventh One" resolves without Special Summoning any monsters. You can only perform the Special Summons from the effect of "Rank-Up-Magic - The Seventh One" once per Duel.
  • A card (1) copies the effect of another card (2) and applies the effects of (2) at resolution: the effect it's still treated as (1)'s name effect[2].
    Example If you activate "Joker's Wild" by sending "Royal Straight" to the Graveyard, you cannot Special Summon "Royal Straight Slasher". When "Joker's Wild" resolves, the sent Spell's effect is applied as the effect of "Joker's Wild". Therefore, the Special Summoned monster would be considered to have been Special Summoned by "Joker's Wild", not by "Royal Straight". As a result, "Royal Straight Slasher", which must be Special Summoned by "Royal Straight", cannot be Special Summoned.
  • If a card (1) says "do [A] at/until [X] turn after this card's activation" ("keeps track" of when (1) was activated), (1)'s effect cannot be copied by activating an effect that copies effects such as "Transaction Rollback"'s Graveyard effect. However, it can be copied by activating a card that copies effects such as by activating a set "Transaction Rollback"[3].
    Example If it is currently either player's Main Phase, you can activate "Trap of Darkness", "Fake Feather" or "Transaction Rollback" by targeting "Extinction on Schedule". If you do, at the end of the third Battle Phase after the respective card was activated, each player must send all cards on their field to the Graveyard normally. However, you cannot activate "Transaction Rollback"'s Graveyard effect by targeting "Extinction on Schedule", and cannot activate "Junk Collector"'s effect by banishing "Extinction on Schedule".
