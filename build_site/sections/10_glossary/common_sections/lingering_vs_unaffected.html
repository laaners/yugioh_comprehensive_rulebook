Lingering Effects vs Unaffected

Depending on the kind of lingering effect and if a card is/becomes unaffected, there can be different outcomes. In the table, ✔️ means that the lingering effect remains applied on the unaffected monster, while ❌ means otherwise.
Lingering Effect (LE) LE applied before the unaffected monster is summoned Unaffected monster on the field, LE applied afterwards Monster becomes unaffected after LE was applied
ATK/DEF modification ✔️[3]
Example Question:
If "Chaos Ancient Gear Giant" is Fusion Summoned by the effect of "Power Bond", which effects are applied?
Answer:
Even if "Chaos Ancient Gear Giant" is Fusion Summoned with the effect of "Power Bond", it will gain ATK equal to its original ATK. During the End Phase of this turn, the effect that inflicts damage to you equal to its original ATK is also applied normally.
("Chaos Ancient Gear Giant" will have 9000 ATK, and you will take 4500 damage during the End Phase of this turn.)
❌[2]
Example Question:
I activate "Spellbook of Power" by targeting a Spellcaster monster, to which the effect of "Forbidden Lance" has previously been applied. In this scenario, what happens to "Spellbook of Power"'s effect that increases the monster's ATK? Additionally, what happens to its effect that adds a "Spellbook" card to your hand?
Furthermore, what happens if "Forbidden Lance" is instead applied to the monster after "Spellbook of Power" has resolved?
Answer:
If you activate "Spellbook of Power" by targeting a Spellcaster that is unaffected by Spell effects, such as due to the effect of "Forbidden Lance", neither effect of "Spellbook of Power" will be applied.
Furthermore, if you activate "Forbidden Lance" by targeting a Spellcaster to which the effect of "Spellbook of Power" has already been applied, the effect of "Spellbook of Power" will remain applied to that monster.

Negate Activated Effects ❌[6]
Example Question:
I activated "Harpie's Feather Storm" during this turn, and its effect that negates any monster effect activated by my opponent is applying. In this situation, if my opponent uses "Forbidden Lance" to make their "Spiritual Beast Cannahawk" unaffected by Spell/Trap effects, and then activates its effect that banishes a "Ritual Beast" card from the Deck, will that effect be negated by "Harpie's Feather Storm"?
Answer:
As in this scenario, when a monster on your opponent's field activates its effect, the effect of "Harpie's Feather Storm" is treated as an effect that is applied to that monster. Therefore, the effect of your opponent's "Spiritual Beast Cannahawk", which is unaffected by Spell/Trap effects, is not negated and resolves normally.
Negate Effects ❌[5]
Example Question:
"Dimensional Barrier" was activated by declaring Xyz Monsters. In this case, if "Forbidden Lance" is activated by targeting an Xyz Monster whose effects are being negated by "Dimensional Barrier", what happens to its effects?
Answer:
The effect of "Dimensional Barrier" negates the effects of the declared monster type as long as they remain face-up on the field, but if "Forbidden Lance" is activated so that a monster becomes unaffected by Spell/Trap effects, its effects are no longer negated. (It does not matter if "Dimensional Barrier" was resolved before or after "Forbidden Lance".)

Piercing Battle Damage ❌[4]
Example Question:
"Forbidden Lance" was activated targeting a "Junk Warrior" affected by "Scrap Fist", making it become unaffected by Spell Cards.
If that "Junk Warrior" attacks other monsters you control during the Battle Phase of that turn, what happens with the applied effects of "Scrap Fist"?
Answer:
In the proposed scenario, the "•" effects that prevent the opponent from activating effects, that double battle Damage and that destroy the monster at the End of the Damage Step are applied normally.
The effects that inflict Piercing battle damage and that prevent the targeted monster from being destroyed in battle are not applied.
(Even if "Forbidden Lance" is activated in a chain to the activation of "Scrap Fist" so that "Junk Warrior" becomes unaffected by Spell Cards this resolves the same way)

❌[1][4]
Example Question:
"Diamond Core of Koa'ki Meiru"'s effect in the Graveyard has been applied this turn. If "Forbidden Lance" is later applied to my "Koa'ki Meiru Overload", and my opponent then activates "Nekroz of Gungnir"'s effect targeting my "Koa'ki Meiru Overload", will it be destroyed?
Answer:
During the turn in which "Diamond Core of Koa'ki Meiru"'s effect in the Graveyard has been applied, "Koa'ki Meiru" monsters you control cannot be destroyed. However, "Forbidden Lance" causes its target to become unaffected by Spell effects, so the effect of "Diamond Core of Koa'ki Meiru" will not be applied to that unaffected monster.
Therefore, in this situation, "Koa'ki Meiru Overload" will be destroyed as normal by the effect of "Nekroz of Gungnir".
