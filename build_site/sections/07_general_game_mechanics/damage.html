  • If damage is taken by an effect, that player's LP is reduced by that amount.
  • If LP is paid for a cost or LP is lost by an effect, this is not treated as taking damage.
    Example When you lose LP by the clause of "Soul Charge" that says "Special Summon them, and if you do, you lose 1000 LP for each monster Special Summoned by this effect.", the effect of "D/D/D Rebel King Leonidas" that says "You take no effect damage." cannot be applied.
  • an effect reads "inflict battle damage" like effects that say "when it attacks a Defense Position monster, inflict piercing battle damage to your opponent", that damage is treated as battle damage.
  • For effects that make the opponent take the battle damage you would take, the battle damage that is exchanged is treated as battle damage.

Battle Damage

Damage inflicted to the LP as a result of monsters battling is called battle damage. To reiterate:

  • The damage taken by a player from a Defense Position monster is also battle damage.
  • The damage from effects that say "inflict battle damage" is not treated as effect damage, and it is treated as battle damage.
References:
  1. OCG Perfect Rulebook 2017 page 192

Inflicting 0 damage is still something that happened

Inflicting 0 damage is still something that happened.

Example Your opponent has just resolved the first effect of their "Destruction Dragon", which was activated by targeting a "Phantom of Chaos" in your Monster Zone. In this situation, a monster you control was destroyed by the effect of your opponent's "Destruction Dragon". Even though the ATK of your destroyed "Phantom of Chaos" is zero, the "if it was a Monster Card, inflict damage to your opponent equal to its original ATK" effect of "Destruction Dragon" is still also applied (you will take 0 damage as a result of this). So you cannot activate a card like "Soul Rope" that needs the last action to happen to be the destruction of the monster, as the last action happened was you taking 0 damage.
