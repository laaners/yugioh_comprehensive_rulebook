Flip Summon

You can change a face-down Defense Position monster into face-up Attack Position, without using a card effect. This is called a Flip Summon[1].

  • When you Flip Summon, you cannot change the monster to face-up Defense Position, only to face-up Attack Position[1].
  • A Monster Card cannot be Flip Summoned in the same turn that it was Set onto the field[1].
  • You cannot use most monsters' effects until they are face-up[1].
  • During the Chain started by an effect that's trying to negate an attempted Flip Summon, the monster that's being Flip Summoned is treated as a face-up monster in the Monster Zone. However, if that monster is an effect monster, its effects are not activated/applied yet[2].
    Example Question:
    The only monster on my side of the field is a face-down Defense Position "Rabidragon". If I Flip Summon "Rabidragon", and my opponent activates "Solemn Judgment", can I chain "Champion's Vigilance" ?
    Answer:
    Yes, you can.
    During the Chain started by an effect that's trying to negate an attempted Flip Summon, the monster that's being Flip Summoned is treated as a face-up monster in the Monster Zone. (However, if that monster is an effect monster, its effects are not activated/applied yet.)
    Therefore, when responding to your opponent's "Solemn Judgment", you control a Level 7 or higher Normal Monster, and the activation conditions for "Champion's Vigilance" are therefore met.
