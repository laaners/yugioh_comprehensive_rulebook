1.1 Problem Solving Card Text (PSCT)

Problem-Solving Card Text (often abbreviated PSCT) is a method of writing card text used for prints released from July 8, 2011 onward. The purpose of Problem-Solving Card Text is to specify card effects to the point that most situations can be resolved by logically interpreting the card text[1].

Problem-Solving Card Text uses specific words with very particular meanings in the context of gameplay. How to interpret the text is important to understanding how to apply card text to the game.

This interpretation can only be applied for cards that use Problem-Solving Card Text. Cards that were printed with earlier text may use some of the same terms or structures, but this does not necessarily mean that they work as if they were Problem-Solving Card Text.

  • All card effects that make a Chain Link include a colon ":" or a semicolon ";" somewhere in their text (possibly both). If a card effect does not use either, it does not make a Chain Link[3].
    • The activation of a Spell or Trap Card always makes a Chain Link, but its effect that resolves with the card itself (if any) does not necessarily use a colon or semicolon[3]. This effect is usually the first effect listed in the card's text.
  • Text before the colon ":" describes activation conditions (limits on when or how often a card or effect can be activated[3][7]). These conditions only need to be met when the card is activated[4].
    • Conditions other than target requirements that have to be met during both activation and resolution are specifically noted in a separate sentence after the effect sentence (e.g. "Zombie Master", "Treeborn Frog")[4].
  • Text before the semicolon ";" (but after the colon, if any) describes anything that happens when that card or effect is activated (such as costs and targeting[3][7]).
  • Built-In Special Summons (other than Synchro, Xyz, Pendulum, or Link) are denoted by parentheses "()" stating the location from which the monster can be Special Summoned[5].
  • Cards with bulleted effects (●) that resolve in the same Chain Link specify whether they resolve simultaneously or sequentially (if sequentially, effects are applied in the order they are listed). In these cases, each effect activated resolves independent of the success of the other(s).
    • The text "in sequence" indicates that the bulleted effects are applied sequentially (e.g. "Gravekeeper's Oracle", "Advance Zone").
    • The text "(simultaneously)" indicates that the bulleted effects are applied simultaneously (e.g. "Ryko, Lightsworn Hunter", "Kunai with Chain").
    • If neither is specified, the effects each make separate Chain Links (e.g. "Metaphys Horus").

1.2 Activating Cards and Effects

1.2.1 Effects that activate and effects that do not start a chain

There are card effects that activate and start a chain, and effects that are applied immediately without starting a chain if some requirements are met.

  • When a Spell or Trap Card is placed face-up on the field, they always start a chain. This is called activating a card.
  • Effects that keep being applied as long as the card remains face-up on the field (Continuous Effects of monsters; Continuous Spells; Field Spells; Equip Spells; Pendulum Effects of Pendulum Monsters; effects of Continuous Traps) are applied without starting a chain in most cases.
References:
  1. OCG Perfect Rulebook 2017 page 130

1.2.2 Activating Effects

There are effects of Spell and Trap Cards that activate and start a chain in the Graveyard or field, other than when the card is activated by placing it face-up on the field. This is called activating an effect.

  • The Ignition Effects/Trigger Effects/Quick Effects of monsters start a chain and constitute activating an effect.
  • Activating the effects of Spells and Traps cannot be negated by effects that negate the activation of cards.

The following effects are examples of cards with effects that activate.

  • Effects that activate on the field after the activation of the card concludes.
  • Effects that can be activated while the card is in the Graveyard or when it is sent to the Graveyard.
References:
  1. OCG Perfect Rulebook 2017 page 130

1.2.3 Activation requirements/condition/legality

An activation condition, also known as its activation requirement, is the set of conditions that must be met in order to activate a card or effect[1].

On cards with Problem-Solving Card Text, the activation condition is written before a colon ":", at the start of a card effect[1].

  • If some effect would be activated, and the resolution of that effect cannot be performed completely, it cannot be activated[2]. Generally, unless a card's effect can be applied before activating the card, that card cannot be activated[4]. Examples:
    • If the opponent does not control any face-up monsters, "Smashing Ground", which has an effect that reads "Destroy the 1 face-up monster your opponent controls that has the highest DEF (your choice, if tied).", cannot be activated[2].
    • If there are no monsters in Attack Position on your opponent's field, "Mirror Force" cannot be activated, even if a Defense Position "Total Defense Shogun" declares an attack[3].
    • Unless there are cards to destroy on the field, you cannot activate "Mystical Space Typhoon", or a previously Set "Raigeki Break". Furthermore, if "Mystical Space Typhoon" is activated, that "Mystical Space Typhoon" itself cannot be targeted as the card to be destroyed[4].
  • Unless otherwise specified, the activation conditions only need to be satisfied when the card is activated, not when it resolves[1].

1.2.3.1 Legality of targeting and paying cost

You cannot choose to use a card or effect that targets unless there is already a card for it to target[1].

  • If, after paying the cost of that card or effect, a new target appears that didn't exist before, you can choose that card as the target of your new effect[1][2].
  • Even if paying a cost of the card or effect you want to activate would create a target for it, you cannot activate it unless the required number of targets already exist[1][2][3].
    Example Question:
    Can I Special Summon the Level 4 or lower "Lightsworn" monster I discarded as a cost to activate the effect of "Lumina, Lightsworn Summoner" with that effect?
    Also, if it is possible, can I activate the effect if there currently is no Level 4 or lower "Lightsworn" monster in my Graveyard that can be Special Summoned?
    Answer:
    You can Special Summon the Level 4 or lower "Lightsworn" monster you discarded as a cost to activate the effect of "Lumina, Lightsworn Summoner" with that effect.
    It cannot be activated if there is no Level 4 or lower "Lightsworn" monster currently in your Graveyard.
  • You control a card with an effect has multiple effects that you can choose (bulleted effects with "●") after paying a cost and the cost is the same. If:
    • The targets for one of the effects, "● effect 1", exist in the correct location, the card's effect can be legally activated and you can choose to resolve "● effect 1"
    • The targets for another one of the effects, "● effect 2", don't exist in the correct location, the card's effect can still be legally activated but you can't choose to resolve "● effect 2"
    But if after paying the cost of that card or effect, a new target appears that didn't exist before that is a target(s) for "● effect 2", and now "● effect 2" has all its targets now, "● effect 2" can be chosen at this time[2].
    Example Question:
    If I detach a material in order to activate the first effect of "Daigusto Emeral", can I target that detached material as one of the cards to shuffle into the Deck?
    Furthermore, if there are only two monsters in the Graveyard, can I detach that material and activate the "●Target 3 monsters in your Graveyard" effect?
    Answer:
    When activating "Daigusto Emeral"'s effect, you first detach a material, then choose which ● bulleted effect to activate, and target an appropriate card.
    Therefore, when you choose which effect to activate, the monster you detached is already in the Graveyard, and can be targeted as one of the monsters to be returned to your Deck.
    Additionally, if the only two monsters in your Graveyard are both Effect Monsters, you cannot activate "Daigusto Emeral"'s effect, as you do not have an appropriate set of target(s) in your Graveyard for either of the ● bulleted effects.
    However, if there are two monsters in your Graveyard, and at least one of them is a non-Effect monster, this means that you could currently choose the "●Target 1 non-Effect Monster in your Graveyard" effect. Therefore, you can activate "Daigusto Emeral"'s effect by detaching a material. If the detached material is sent to your Graveyard, and this results in you having 3 monsters in your Graveyard, you can then choose the "●Target 3 monsters in your Graveyard" effect as the effect to activate.

1.2.3.2 Legality of 0 ATK/DEF modifiers

A Monster's (1) ATK/DEF is already 0. A card's (2) effect which involves:

  • Reducing (1)'s ATK/DEF can be activated and will resolve successfully[1].
    Example Question:
    Can the first effect of "Malacoda, Netherlord of the Burning Abyss" be activated by targeting a monster with 0 ATK and DEF?
    Answer:
    You can target a monster with 0 ATK and DEF with the effect of "Malacoda, Netherlord of the Burning Abyss". For example, you can activate this effect by sending "Libic, Malebranche of the Burning Abyss" (ATK 1300/DEF 700) from your hand to the Graveyard, targeting "Battle Fader" (ATK 0/DEF 0). "Battle Fader" will still have 0 ATK and DEF, but because the effect of "Malacoda, Netherlord of the Burning Abyss" was applied, even if "Battle Fader" is then equipped with "United We Stand" and its ATK/DEF are increased, you must recalculate its ATK/DEF and include the decreased values.
  • Halving (1)'s ATK/DEF can be activated and will resolve successfully[2].
    Example Question:
    Can "TGX1-HL" be activated by targeting a "T.G." monster whose ATK and DEF are zero?
    Answer:
    You can activate "TGX1-HL" by targeting a "T.G." monster whose current ATK and DEF are both zero. If you do, the effect that destroys a Spell/Trap on the field is applied as usual.
  • Setting (1)'s ATK/DEF to 0 cannot be activated[3].
    Example Question:
    Can I target a monster with 0 ATK with the effect of "Code Radiator" that was sent from the hand or field to the Graveyard as material for the Link Summon of a ""Code Talker"" monster?
    Answer:
    You can target a monster with 0 ATK, as long as the "its effects are negated" effect can be applied to that monster.
    The "its effects are negated" effect can primarily be applied to Effect Monsters whose effects are not yet negated. Additionally, Gemini monsters which are treated as Normal Monsters by their "This card is treated as a Normal Monster while face-up on the field or in the Graveyard." effect are also monsters to which the "its effects are negated" effect can be applied.
  • Switching (1)'s ATK/DEF (when both are 0) can be activated and will resolve successfully[4].
    Example Question:
    Can a monster with 0 ATK and DEF be targeted to activated the Monster Effect of "Performage Mirror Conductor"?
    Answer:
    Yes, it can.
    Even in that case, the parts of the effect that swap the ATK and DEF and that inflict 500 damage resolves normally.

1.2.3.3 Legality of adding then discarding/sending

A card's (1) effect which involves adding a card (2) with a certain property and then discarding/sending a card (3) with (2)'s same property, (1) can be activated even if there are no cards of with (2)'s property in your hand at the moment of (1)'s activation.

Example Question:
There are currently no Dragon monsters and no Winged Beast monsters in my hand. Can I activate the effect of "Dragunity Knight - Gae Dearg"?
Answer:
You can activate the effect of "Dragunity Knight - Gae Dearg" even if there are no Dragon monsters and no Winged Beast monsters in your hand. If you do this, you will first add a Dragon or Winged Beast from your Deck to your hand, then discard that same monster.

1.2.3.4 Legality of change control

If you have no available Main Monster Zones, you cannot activate an effect that would take control of an opponent's monster[1]. Vice versa, you cannot activate an effect that gives control of a monster if your opponent has no available Main Monster Zones[2].

Example Question:
If there are no available Main Monster Zones on my opponent's field, can I activate the effect of "Euler's Circuit" that gives my opponent control of a "Tindangle" monster on my field?
Answer:
The effect of "Euler's Circuit" that gives your opponent control of a "Tindangle" monster on your field cannot be activated if your opponent has no available Main Monster Zones. Furthermore, when resolving that effect of "Euler's Circuit", if your opponent has no available Main Monster Zones, the targeted "Tindangle" monster cannot change control and it is destroyed. (That monster is not treated as being destroyed by a card effect, so even if the destroyed monster is "Tindangle Hound", its effect that activates when it is destroyed by battle or card effect cannot be activated.)

1.2.3.5 Legality of delayed effects

Effects of this kind can be activated even if resolution of that effect cannot be performed completely at the time of the activation[1]. Examples:

  • Question:
    Can I activate the effect of "Infernoble Knight Captain Roland" that can be activated when it is Synchro Summoned if neither an Equip Spell nor a Warrior monster is in my Deck?
    Answer:
    You can still activate the effect of "Infernoble Knight Captain Roland" in question even if there is no Equip Spell or Warrior monster (or neither of them) in your Deck.
    In this case, when performing the effect during the End Phase, you will first send an Equip Spell from you Deck to the Graveyard if possible. (If there is no Equip Spell in your Deck, neither that part nor the part of adding a Warrior monster to your hand is applied.)
    Next, after you sent an Equip Spell from your Deck to the Graveyard, if there is a Warrior monster in your Deck at this point, you will add it to your hand. (If there is no Warrior monster in your Deck, the effect is completed by sending an Equip Spell to the Graveyard.)[1]
  • Question:
    Can I activate "Future Fusion" if I have no cards in my Extra Deck?
    Answer:
    You can activate "Future Fusion" even if you have no cards in your Extra Deck. Afterward, if you still have no cards in your Extra Deck when the first effect of "Future Fusion" resolves, you cannot reveal a Fusion Monster from your Extra Deck so the effect is not applied[2].

1.2.3.6 Legality of destroying to summon

An effect that destroys a card(s) on your side of the field to summon can be activated even when all of your Main Monster Zones are full, but the card(s) you have to destroy must be one monster(s) in your Main Monster Zone.

Example Question:
If there are 5 monsters in my Main Monster Zones, can I activate the effect of "Crystron Prasiortle", "Crystron Smiger", "Crystron Thystvern", or "Crystron Rosenix" that destroys a face-up card on my field and Special Summons a "Crystron" Tuner monster from my Deck?
Answer:
The effect of a monster such as "Crystron Prasiortle" Special Summons a "Crystron" Tuner monster from your Deck, so if there are already 5 monsters in your Main Monster Zones, you can only activate the effect by targeting a face-up monster in your Main Monster Zone. (Face-up cards in your Spell & Trap Zones, Field Zone, or the Extra Monster Zone cannot be targeted.)

References:

1.2.3.7 Legality of effect negation

  • The "its effects are negated" effect can primarily be applied to Effect Monsters whose effects are not yet negated[1][2].
    Example Question:
    If the targeted monster's effects are already negated when resolving "Joruri-P.U.N.K. Dangerous Gabu", what happens?
    Answer:
    In that case, the monster's effects cannot be negated until the end of the turn. Therefore, you also do not gain Life Points equal to that monster's original ATK.
  • Effects negation can apply to Effect Monsters without any effect like "Mara of the Nordic Alfar"[3].
    Example Question:
    If a "Dark Lucius LV6" that was Special Summoned by the effect of "Dark Lucius LV4" destroys an opponent's "Mara of the Nordic Alfar" by battle, will its effect that negates the effects of monsters it destroys by battle be applied? Furthermore, during the Standby Phase of my next turn, can I activate the effect of "Dark Lucius LV6" that Special Summons a "Dark Lucius LV8" from my hand or Deck?
    Answer:
    "Mara of the Nordic Alfar" is an Effect Monster, even though it has no monster effects. Therefore, even if "Dark Lucius LV6" destroys an opponent's "Mara of the Nordic Alfar" by battle, the effect that negates the effects of monsters it destroys by battle is applied normally.
    Furthermore, the effect of "Dark Lucius LV6" that Special Summons a "Dark Lucius LV8" can be activated during your next Standby Phase.

1.2.3.8 Legality of Equip Cards

Equip Cards with impossible Continuous Effects can be activated. A Link Monster can be equipped with "Horn of Light" or "Rod of Silence - Kay'est". However, since Link Monsters do not have DEF, the effect of increasing DEF is not applied. In the case of "Rod of Silence - Kay'est" , the effect that negates other Spells' effects targeting the equipped monster and destroys them is applied normally.

1.2.3.9 Legality of gaining 0 LP

A card's effect which gains LP can be activated even if it will gain 0 LP and will resolve successfully[1].

Example Question:
"Predaplant Moray Nepenthes" is equipped with a "Performapal Life Swordsman" by its second effect. In this situation, can the third effect of "Predaplant Moray Nepenthes" be activated by targeting the equipped "Performapal Life Swordsman"?
Answer:
If "Predaplant Moray Nepenthes" is equipped with a monster by its own effect, even if that monster has 0 ATK such as "Performapal Life Swordsman", you can also activate the effect that destroys an equipped monster and increases your Life Points by its ATK. In this case, the equipped monster is destroyed normally, but you gain no Life Points.

1.2.3.10 Legality under "Macro Cosmos"

  • When paying costs that say "discard to the Graveyard", if "Macro Cosmos" or something similar is being applied, so that cards that would be sent to the Graveyard are banished, the activation of cards that discard to the Graveyard as a cost cannot be performed. Costs that simply say "discard" can be paid. In that case, the cards are not sent to the Graveyard and are banished as a result[1].
    Example While the effect of "Macro Cosmos" is being applied, the effect of "Hecatrice" that says "You can discard this card to the Graveyard to add 1 "Valhalla, Hall of the Fallen" from your Deck to your hand." cannot be activated, but the effect of "Ghost Reaper & Winter Cherries" that says "During either player's turn, if your opponent controls more monsters than you do: You can discard this card;" can be activated.
    Furthermore, cards banished in this way are treated as being banished by a card effect, even if they were discarded as cost[2].
    Example Question:
    I activate "Super Polymerization", or activate "Fallen of Albaz"' effect, by discarding "Despian Tragedy" as a cost.
    After this activation resolves, can the effect of "Despian Tragedy", which activates if it is sent to the Graveyard or banished by a card effect, be activated?
    Answer:
    In this case, "Despian Tragedy" was discarded as a cost to activate an effect. It was not sent to the Graveyard by a card effect. Thus, the effect of "Despian Tragedy" cannot be activated.
    The effect of "Despian Tragedy" is an effect that can be activated when "Despian Tragedy" is sent to the Graveyard by a card effect, or is banished.
    However, if the situation above occurs while the effect of "Macro Cosmos" is applying, the "Despian Tragedy" will not be discarded to the Graveyard. Instead, it will be banished by the effect of "Macro Cosmos". As "Despian Tragedy" was banished by a card effect, the effect of "Despian Tragedy" can then be activated.
  • For costs that read "send to the Graveyard;", those monsters must be sent to the Graveyard in order to activate the effect. If "Macro Cosmos" or similar is being applied, so that the cards that would be sent to the Graveyard are banished instead and so that it is not possible to send cards to the Graveyard, activations that send cards to the Graveyard as a cost cannot be performed[1].
  • For costs that tribute, if "Macro Cosmos" or something similar is being applied, so that monsters that would be sent to the Graveyard are banished, you can still Tribute monsters. In that case, the Tributed monsters are not sent to the Graveyard and are banished instead[1].
References:
  1. OCG Perfect Rulebook 2017 pages 131-133
  2. YGOrg Database of Official OCG Rulings: "Despian Tragedy", "Fallen of Albaz", "Super Polymerization", "Macro Cosmos"

1.2.3.11 Legality under "Dimensional Fissure"

When the effect "any Monster sent to the GY is banished instead" is applying you:

  • Cannot activate a card or effect that requires you to send a Monster to the Graveyard as a cost[1].
  • Cannot activate a card or effect that send cards from the top of your Deck to the Graveyard as cost as there may be monster cards among them[1].
  • Can activate a card or effect that requires you to send a Monster Card not treated as a Monster (for example treated as an Equip Spell Card) to the Graveyard as a cost[2].
    Example Question:
    If the effect of "Dimensional Fissure" is applied, you cannot send monsters to the Graveyard as a cost to activate a card or an effect, but is it possible to send a card such a Union Monster treated as an Equip Spell Card to the Graveyard as a cost to activate the effect of "Guardian Eatos"?
    Answer:
    Even if the effect of "Dimensional Fissure" is applied, you can send cards such as Union Monsters treated as Equip Spell Cards to the Graveyard as a cost to activate the effect of "Guardian Eatos".
  • Can activate a card or effect that requires you to send a Trap Card summoned as a monster (either not treated as a Trap or still treated as a Trap) to the Graveyard as a cost[3].
    Example Question:
    I control an "Angel Statue - Azurune", which was Special Summoned by its own effect, and the effect of "Dimensional Fissure" (or "Ryu-Ge Ascension Gate of Wyrmhaze") is applying. Can I activate "Angel Statue - Azurune"'s effect by "sending 1 Continuous Trap in your Monster Zone that was Special Summoned from the Spell & Trap Zone to the Graveyard"?
    Answer:
    Yes, you can.

1.2.4 Cost

To pay a cost is an action required by a player in order to activate a card, an effect, or to Summon a monster. Tributing to Tribute Summon a Level 5 monster is an example of a cost. Other common costs are discarding, paying LP, or banishing cards. You have to pay any costs before you declare the activation of the card[3].

On cards with Problem-Solving Card Text, the cost is written before a semicolon ";", at the start of a card effect or after the activation condition[1].

  • Costs must be performed at the time the card is activated, and even if the activation or effect of the card is negated, they are not refunded[2][3].
  • If the player cannot suffice the costs, they cannot activate the card[2].
  • Costs are not treated as card effects[2].
  • Unless stated otherwise, costs can only be paid with cards in your possession[2].
  • Even if a card's effect is being negated (such as by "Skill Drain"), it is still possible to activate its effects (which would require paying the cost)[1].

1.2.4.1 Discard for cost

Examples
D.D. Crow

(Quick Effect): You can discard this card to the GY, then target 1 card in your opponent's GY; banish that target.
D.D.R. - Different Dimension Reincarnation

Discard 1 card, then target 1 of your banished monsters; Special Summon it in Attack Position, and equip it with this card. When this card leaves the field, destroy that monster.
Cursed Seal of the Forbidden Spell

When a Spell Card is activated: Discard 1 Spell; negate the activation, and if you do, destroy it, and if you do that, your opponent cannot activate Spell Cards with that name for the rest of this Duel.

For costs that read "discard [X]", cards from the hand must be discarded from the hand in order to activate the effects. Cards that are discarded are treated as being discarded from the hand and as being sent to the Graveyard as a result[1].

1.2.4.4 Tribute for cost

Tributing is sending a monster you control to the Graveyard. You can Tribute a face-up or face-down monster, unless otherwise specified. Tributing a monster is one possible cost for Summoning a monster or activating an effect[2]. In order to activate effects that read "Tribute [X];", those monsters need to be Tributed.   • The Tributed monsters are not treated as being destroyed. As a result, they are treated as being sent to the Graveyard[1].
  • Even if the monsters that would be Tributed need to meet certain requirements, face-down monsters on the field can be sent to the Graveyard[1].

  • If "Macro Cosmos" or something similar is being applied, so that monsters that would be sent to the Graveyard are banished, you can still Tribute monsters. In that case, the Tributed monsters are not sent to the Graveyard and are banished instead[1].

1.2.5 Target

Targeting is the action of designating which specific cards are affected or used by card effects at the activation of a card effect[1].

In Problem-Solving Card Text, cards and effects that target always explicitly state that they target: a card targets if and only if it uses the word "target"[1].

  • Cards can be targeted if they are on the field, in the Graveyard, or banished[1]. Cards in the hand[1], Main Deck[1], Extra Deck[3], or attached as Xyz Material[1] cannot be targeted.
  • You cannot choose to use a card or effect that targets unless there is a card for it to target[5].
  • Targets are selected when the effect is activated. Even if the effect is negated, it is not forgotten that they have been targeted. However, if the activation of that effect is negated, the cards are no longer targeted[2].
  • If a card was targeted by a card or effect, but when the effect resolves that target has changed location, the target is no longer valid and cannot be affected as the target. The player does not get to change their target or select another target at resolution[2].
  • If an effect is activated targeting 2 or more cards, and afterwards (before the resolution) 1 of them has been moved to a different location, the effect is applied, except if some particular requirement must be met[2].
    Example "Icarus Attack" was activated targeting 2 cards on the field. If 1 of the targeted cards is sent to the Graveyard by another card effect in a chain, the effect of "Icarus Attack" that says "destroy them." is applied on the remaining card.
  • Cards that have an effect that says they cannot be targeted for effects cannot be targeted to activate effects that target. After a card has been targeted to activate an effect, if that card is affected by an effect that makes it unable to be targeted, since it has already been targeted, the effect is still applied afterwards[2].
    Example When the opponent activates "Compulsory Evacuation Device", which says "Target 1 monster on the field; return that target to the hand." targeting a monster, if you activate "Forbidden Dress" targeting the same monster, that monster is affected by the effect of "Forbidden Dress" that says "until the end of this turn, that target loses 600 ATK, but cannot be targeted or destroyed by other card effects.", but since it has already been targeted for the effect of "Compulsory Evacuation Device", the effect resolves afterwards and it is returned to the hand.
  • Equip Spell Cards always target, but activating an effect that equips does not implicitly target[4].
    Example Question:
    The Quick Effect of "Amazement Ambassador Bufo" was activated by targeting an equipped Trap you control.
    When that effect resolves, can I equip that Trap to my opponent's "The First Darklord", even though it cannot be targeted by card effects?
    Answer:
    Yes, you can.
    That effect of "Amazement Ambassador Bufo" does not require you to target any monsters. Thus, you can equip your Trap to a "The First Darklord" that cannot be targeted by your card effects.

1.2.5.1 That target vs It

The terms "that target" or "those targets" and "it" or "they/them" have specific meanings in the resolution of the effect[1].

  • "That target" and "those targets" indicate that targets must still meet the target requirements at resolution to be affected[1].
    Example "Zombie Master" uses "that target". If "that target" it isn't a Level 4 or lower Zombie-Type monster in the Graveyard anymore, it is not Summoned. For example you targeted a non-Zombie-Type Monster while "Zombie World" is face-up on the field, but "Zombie World" is destroyed before the effect of "Zombie Master" resolves.
  • "It" and "they/them" indicate that the targets do not need to meet the target requirements at resolution, only when targeted[1].
    Example "Adreus, Keeper of Armageddon" says "destroy it". Even if the target was flipped face-down, or shifted to your side of the field, it's still destroyed.
  • "Both (those) targets" and "all [X] targets" indicate that all targets must still meet the target requirements at resolution for any of them to be affected. If even one of the targets is no longer eligible, then the whole effect does not apply[1].

1.2.5.2 Legality of targeting and paying cost

You cannot choose to use a card or effect that targets unless there is already a card for it to target[1].

  • If, after paying the cost of that card or effect, a new target appears that didn't exist before, you can choose that card as the target of your new effect[1][2].
  • Even if paying a cost of the card or effect you want to activate would create a target for it, you cannot activate it unless the required number of targets already exist[1][2][3].
    Example Question:
    Can I Special Summon the Level 4 or lower "Lightsworn" monster I discarded as a cost to activate the effect of "Lumina, Lightsworn Summoner" with that effect?
    Also, if it is possible, can I activate the effect if there currently is no Level 4 or lower "Lightsworn" monster in my Graveyard that can be Special Summoned?
    Answer:
    You can Special Summon the Level 4 or lower "Lightsworn" monster you discarded as a cost to activate the effect of "Lumina, Lightsworn Summoner" with that effect.
    It cannot be activated if there is no Level 4 or lower "Lightsworn" monster currently in your Graveyard.
  • You control a card with an effect has multiple effects that you can choose (bulleted effects with "●") after paying a cost and the cost is the same. If:
    • The targets for one of the effects, "● effect 1", exist in the correct location, the card's effect can be legally activated and you can choose to resolve "● effect 1"
    • The targets for another one of the effects, "● effect 2", don't exist in the correct location, the card's effect can still be legally activated but you can't choose to resolve "● effect 2"
    But if after paying the cost of that card or effect, a new target appears that didn't exist before that is a target(s) for "● effect 2", and now "● effect 2" has all its targets now, "● effect 2" can be chosen at this time[2].
    Example Question:
    If I detach a material in order to activate the first effect of "Daigusto Emeral", can I target that detached material as one of the cards to shuffle into the Deck?
    Furthermore, if there are only two monsters in the Graveyard, can I detach that material and activate the "●Target 3 monsters in your Graveyard" effect?
    Answer:
    When activating "Daigusto Emeral"'s effect, you first detach a material, then choose which ● bulleted effect to activate, and target an appropriate card.
    Therefore, when you choose which effect to activate, the monster you detached is already in the Graveyard, and can be targeted as one of the monsters to be returned to your Deck.
    Additionally, if the only two monsters in your Graveyard are both Effect Monsters, you cannot activate "Daigusto Emeral"'s effect, as you do not have an appropriate set of target(s) in your Graveyard for either of the ● bulleted effects.
    However, if there are two monsters in your Graveyard, and at least one of them is a non-Effect monster, this means that you could currently choose the "●Target 1 non-Effect Monster in your Graveyard" effect. Therefore, you can activate "Daigusto Emeral"'s effect by detaching a material. If the detached material is sent to your Graveyard, and this results in you having 3 monsters in your Graveyard, you can then choose the "●Target 3 monsters in your Graveyard" effect as the effect to activate.

1.2.6 Effect Resolution

  • In most cases, when the rules and the written resolution of the effects are in contradiction, the written card text takes precedence[1].
  • If the effect of a card is activated and that card is destroyed or otherwise moved to a different location, in most cases the effect resolves[1].
  • If there are multiple parts of an effect for a single effect of a card, they are resolved in the order in which they are written. Also, in most cases, if 1 of the parts of the effect cannot resolve, none of the latter parts are resolved (see conjunctives)[1].
  • It is not possible to make choices that would cause an activated effect to be unable to be resolved[1].
    Example When the effect of "Lunalight Reincarnation Dance" that says "Add up to 2 "Lunalight" monsters from your Deck to your hand." it is not possible to choose to resolve the effect by adding 0 cards.
  • If (at the time of the resolution of a single effect) both players need to resolve that effect simultaneously, the turn player performs the resolution first[1].
    Example When the effect of "Hand Destruction" that says "Each player sends 2 cards from their hand to the Graveyard, then each player draws 2 cards." resolves, first, the turn player sends 2 cards from their hand to the Graveyard, then the opposite player sends 2 cards from their own hand to the Graveyard. Then, the turn player draws 2 cards, and finally the opposite player draws 2 cards.
  • The effects of other cards cannot be activated during the resolution of an effect. If the activation timing for a mandatory effect is met during the resolution of an effect, it is activated after that effect finishes resolving[1].
  • At the time an effect resolves, if the targets for that effect or the cards that it would affect no longer exist, the effect does not resolve[2].
  • There are cases in which if an effect that needs to meet requirements at activation or that affects only some particular cards is activated, and later the card that activated, the targets for the effect, or the cards that it would affect no longer meet some particular requirements, that effect is not applied[2]. Examples:
    • For the effect of "Zombie Master" that says "Once per turn: You can send 1 monster from your hand to the Graveyard, then target 1 Level 4 or lower Zombie-Type monster in either player's Graveyard; Special Summon that target. This card must be face-up on the field to activate and to resolve this effect.", if "Zombie Master" is no longer face-up as a result of a card effect being activated in a chain or something similar, the Special Summoning effect does not resolve.
    • For the effect of "High Tide on Fire Island" that says "Apply the appropriate effects, in sequence, depending on the Attributes of monsters you control at that time.", if there are no WATER or FIRE monsters as a result of a card effect being activated in a chain or something similar, the effect does not resolve.
  • Even if an effect fails to resolve properly, the activation timing is treated as if the resolution is performed. As a result, effects cannot be activated during the time in which that failed resolution is performed[1].
  • Effects resolve where they were activated[3].
    Example Question:
    This turn, my opponent activated and successfully resolved the effect of a previously Set "Infinite Impermanence" and its effect that negates all other Spell/Trap effects in the same column for the rest of this turn is applied.
    In this situation, if I activate the effect of my "Wonder Wand" that draws 2 cards by sending itself and the equipped monster I control to the Graveyard as a cost, will that effect be negated?
    Answer:
    If the effect of "Infinite Impermanence" that negates all other Spell/Trap effects in the same column for the rest of this turn is applied, not only will Spell/Trap Cards activated in the same column be negated, but also the effects of Spell/Trap Cards activated in the same column.
    In the given situation, "Wonder Wand" itself is sent to the Graveyard as a cost to activate its effect, but the effect was activated in the same column as the previously resolved "Infinite Impermanence", so that effect will be negated.

2.1.2 Special cases

2.1.2.1 Applying a special non-activated effect that is always applied last

Certain effects that set a monster's current ATK/DEF continuously are always reapplied last as new modifiers are introduced, breaking some of the rules established above. Even though they can be described in the same way (non-activated effects that set ATK/DEF to a determined value) it is not always clear why they work differently[1].

For these special cases, no matter what the effects currently in play are, they are always applied last[2].

  • If a new effect is applied, reapply those effects right afterwards[2].
  • When those effects stop applying, reapply everything, including lingering effects (1,3,5)[2].
Examples
Super Crashbug

"Number 17: Leviathan Dragon" and "Super Crashbug" are on the field. The effect of "Super Crashbug" switches the ATK/DEF of "Number 17: Leviathan Dragon" to be 0/2000. If the effect of "Number 17: Leviathan Dragon" is activated, its ATK is recalculated with the effect of "Super Crashbug" being applied last. The ATK/DEF become 0/2500, not 500/2000. If "Super Crashbug" leaves the field, the ATK/DEF of "Number 17: Leviathan Dragon" becomes 2500/0[1].

The Wicked Dreadroot

"Red-Eyes Black Dragon" is equipped with "Megamorph", and "The Wicked Dreadroot" is on the field, which halves the current ATK/DEF of all other monsters on the field. The ATK of "Red-Eyes Black Dragon" is either 600 or 2400, while its DEF is 1000[1].

"Black Garden" and "The Wicked Dreadroot" are on the field. If "Aleister the Invoker" is Normal Summoned, the effect of "The Wicked Dreadroot" is immediately applied, so the ATK/DEF of "Aleister the Invoker" become 500/900. The effect of "Black Garden" then activates, and halves the ATK of "Aleister the Invoker" to 250. Now, the effect of The "The Wicked Dreadroot" is reapplied to the newly-determined value, and halves it further to 125. (The DEF remains 900). If "The Wicked Dreadroot" leaves the field, the ATK of "Aleister the Invoker" becomes 250 again[1].

The Wicked Avatar

"The Wicked Avatar" and "Salamangreat Heatleo" are on the field, and the ATK/DEF of "The Wicked Avatar" are each 2400. If the effect of "Salamangreat Heatleo" is activated, targeting a "Knightmare Phoenix" in the Graveyard and "The Wicked Avatar" on the field, the ATK of "The Wicked Avatar" becomes 1900, but immediately reverts to 2400. During this turn, if the effects of "The Wicked Avatar" are negated, its ATK will become 1900 again and its DEF becomes 0[1][3].

"Water Dragon", "Apple Magician Girl", and "Chocolate Magician Girl" are all on the field, and the ATK of "Apple Magician Girl" is 0 due to the effect of "Water Dragon". If "Magicians Unite" is activated, targeting "Apple Magician Girl", its ATK becomes 3000, but the effect of "Water Dragon" is immediately reapplied and makes the ATK become 0 again. If "Water Dragon" is destroyed this turn, the ATK of "Apple Magician Girl" becomes 3000 again[1].

If "Clear Vice Dragon" attacks "Red-Eyes Black Dragon", "Clear Vice Dragon" will have 4800 ATK during damage calculation. If "TA.I. Strike" is activated during damage calculation, the ATK of "Clear Vice Dragon" becomes 2400, but the effect is reapplied and becomes 4800 again[1] .

The Pendulum Effect of "Performapal Turn Toad" is applied to the "Option Token", which switches its ATK/DEF from 1200/1000 to 1000/1200. 2.1.2.2 Activated effects that work like non-activated effects: Set own ATK/DEF on Summon

A monster's effect that sets its own current or original ATK/DEF upon being Summoned is generally considered a Continuous Effect, because it does not activate. However, these effects behave more like activated effects with respect to the interaction rules covered above. They are only applied once when the Summon is successful, and are not continuously-applying thereafter. "Naturia Beetle" is similar as well, although its effect is not applied upon being Summoned[1].

  • A monster (1) whose effect reads "The ATK of this card becomes the combined original ATK of the Tributed monsters", if it is Summoned by tributing a monster (2) on the field whose original ATK was set by another card (3), (1)'s ATK will be (2)'s printed ATK even though (2)'s original ATK differs from its printed ATK due to (3)[2].
    Example Question:
    A "Beast King Barbaros" which was Normal Summoned without Tributes, with an original ATK of 1900 - or a "Fusilier Dragon, the Dual-Mode Beast" which was Normal Summoned without Tributes, with an original ATK of 1400 - is Tributed to Tribute Summon "Fog King".
    What is "Fog King"'s ATK?
    Answer:
    "Fog King"'s ATK will become the combined printed ATK of the monsters Tributed for its Tribute Summon.
    Therefore, even if the original ATK of the "Beast King Barbaros" on the field is treated as 1900, "Fog King"'s ATK will become the 3000 printed on "Beast King Barbaros".
Examples
Apollousa, Bow of the Goddess

"Apollousa, Bow of the Goddess" has an original ATK of 3200 by its own effect, and has used its effect to decrease its ATK by 800 twice, for a total decrease of 1600 ATK. If the effect of "Shrink" is applied to "Apollousa, Bow of the Goddess", its original ATK becomes 1600, and the decrease of 1600 by its own effect is reapplied, making its current ATK 0. At the end of the turn, the original ATK of "Apollousa, Bow of the Goddess" becomes 0[1][3].

Naturia Beetle

"Naturia Beetle" has its ATK/DEF halved by the effect of "Blackwing - Gale the Whirlwind", so its ATK/DEF are 200/900. If a Spell Card is activated, the effect of "Naturia Beetle" is applied and its original ATK/DEF switch. Its current ATK/DEF will be 1800/400, meaning the effect of "Blackwing - Gale the Whirlwind" is no longer applied[1].

2.1.2.3 Activated effects that work like non-activated effects

Conversely, there are activated effects that behave like non-activated effects, such as "Spiritual Beast Apelio" and "Aroma Garden". The activated effect's resolution is to begin applying a non-activated effect, which then takes effect for a certain amount of time. These cards have effects that activate and resolve, but the applied increase functions like a non-activated effect[1].

The ATK of "Ritual Beast Ulti-Gaiapelio" becomes 0, and damage calculation occurs with that value. After damage calculation concludes, the effect of "Kazejin" stops applying and the effect of "Spiritual Beast Apelio" reapplies, so the ATK of "Ritual Beast Ulti-Gaiapelio" becomes 3700[1].

2.1.3 Legality of 0 ATK/DEF modifiers

A Monster's (1) ATK/DEF is already 0. A card's (2) effect which involves:

  • Reducing (1)'s ATK/DEF can be activated and will resolve successfully[1].
    Example Question:
    Can the first effect of "Malacoda, Netherlord of the Burning Abyss" be activated by targeting a monster with 0 ATK and DEF?
    Answer:
    You can target a monster with 0 ATK and DEF with the effect of "Malacoda, Netherlord of the Burning Abyss". For example, you can activate this effect by sending "Libic, Malebranche of the Burning Abyss" (ATK 1300/DEF 700) from your hand to the Graveyard, targeting "Battle Fader" (ATK 0/DEF 0). "Battle Fader" will still have 0 ATK and DEF, but because the effect of "Malacoda, Netherlord of the Burning Abyss" was applied, even if "Battle Fader" is then equipped with "United We Stand" and its ATK/DEF are increased, you must recalculate its ATK/DEF and include the decreased values.
  • Halving (1)'s ATK/DEF can be activated and will resolve successfully[2].
    Example Question:
    Can "TGX1-HL" be activated by targeting a "T.G." monster whose ATK and DEF are zero?
    Answer:
    You can activate "TGX1-HL" by targeting a "T.G." monster whose current ATK and DEF are both zero. If you do, the effect that destroys a Spell/Trap on the field is applied as usual.
  • Setting (1)'s ATK/DEF to 0 cannot be activated[3].
    Example Question:
    Can I target a monster with 0 ATK with the effect of "Code Radiator" that was sent from the hand or field to the Graveyard as material for the Link Summon of a ""Code Talker"" monster?
    Answer:
    You can target a monster with 0 ATK, as long as the "its effects are negated" effect can be applied to that monster.
    The "its effects are negated" effect can primarily be applied to Effect Monsters whose effects are not yet negated. Additionally, Gemini monsters which are treated as Normal Monsters by their "This card is treated as a Normal Monster while face-up on the field or in the Graveyard." effect are also monsters to which the "its effects are negated" effect can be applied.
  • Switching (1)'s ATK/DEF (when both are 0) can be activated and will resolve successfully[4].
    Example Question:
    Can a monster with 0 ATK and DEF be targeted to activated the Monster Effect of "Performage Mirror Conductor"?
    Answer:
    Yes, it can.
    Even in that case, the parts of the effect that swap the ATK and DEF and that inflict 500 damage resolves normally.

2.3.2 Triggers for discarding for hand size for a second time

If you discard cards for hand size at the end of your End Phase, you can only activate effects on the Chain immediately following this. Even if, at some point after this first Chain, some other effect meets its activation condition, you cannot activate that effect.

    I chained "Book of Moon" to the activation of the effect of "Judgment Dragon", and then further chained "Forbidden Chalice". In this case, the Chain resolving in reverse order causes the "Judgment Dragon" that was affected by "Forbidden Chalice" to change to face-down Defense Position. Is the effect of "Judgment Dragon" negated?
    Also, if I activated "Interdimensional Matter Transporter" in a chain to the effect of "Judgment Dragon", would the effect be negated?
    Also, if I activated "Compulsory Evacuation Device" in a chain to the effect of "Judgment Dragon", would the effect be negated?
    Answer:
    Due to the Chain resolving in reverse order, the effect of "Forbidden Chalice" will not be applied once "Judgment Dragon" changes to face-down Defense Position due to the effect of "Book of Moon", so the effect of "Judgment Dragon" will not be negated, and all other cards on the field will be destroyed.
    Also, if "Judgment Dragon" is banished by the effect of "Interdimensional Matter Transporter" as the Chain resolves in reverse order, its effect will be negated.
    Also, if "Judgment Dragon" is returned to the hand by the effect of "Compulsory Evacuation Device" as the Chain resolves in reverse order, its effect will be negated.

Then an effect that negates monster's effects (3) is chained to negate (1). 2.4.1 Legality of adding then discarding/sending

A card's (1) effect which involves adding a card (2) with a certain property and then discarding/sending a card (3) with (2)'s same property, (1) can be activated even if there are no cards of with (2)'s property in your hand at the moment of (1)'s activation.

Example Question:
There are currently no Dragon monsters and no Winged Beast monsters in my hand. Can I activate the effect of "Dragunity Knight - Gae Dearg"?
Answer:
You can activate the effect of "Dragunity Knight - Gae Dearg" even if there are no Dragon monsters and no Winged Beast monsters in your hand. If you do this, you will first add a Dragon or Winged Beast from your Deck to your hand, then discard that same monster.

2.5 Return/Shuffle to the Deck

Examples
Madolche Queen Tiaramisu

2 Level 4 "Madolche" monsters
Once per turn: You can detach 1 material from this card, then target up to 2 "Madolche" card(s) in your GY; shuffle those cards into the Deck, also, after that, shuffle cards your opponent controls into the Deck, up to the number of "Madolche" cards returned.

Foolish Return

Target 1 card in your opponent's GY; shuffle it into the Deck.
Drowning Mirror Force

When an opponent's monster declares a direct attack: Shuffle all your opponent's Attack Position monsters into the Deck.
  • If cards are returned to the Deck, that card is added face-down to the Deck and is shuffled. However, if a card is returned to a specified location like the top or bottom of the Deck, the shuffling is not performed[1].
  • If, at the timing a card effect can be activated, that card has been returned to the Deck, in most cases that effect cannot be activated[1].
  • An activating Quick-Play Spell Card (1) cannot be returned to the Deck by an effect (2) in chain that does so. This is the case either when (1) is a Chain Link lower than (2) or vice versa: (1) is not a legal target for (2)[2].
    Example Question:
    My opponent has 1 Set card in their Spell & Trap Zone.
    In this situation, I Ritual Summon "Shinobaroness Peacock". I then activate its effect that returns up to 3 of my opponent's Spell/Trap Cards to the Deck, then Special Summons a Level 4 or lower Spirit monster from my Deck.
    My opponent activates their Set card in response, which is a "Mystical Space Typhoon". How does the effect of "Shinobaroness Peacock" resolve?
    Answer:
    When resolving the effect of "Shinobaroness Peacock", a "Mystical Space Typhoon" that was activated in response cannot be returned to the Deck.
    In this scenario, if there are no other Spell/Trap Cards on my opponent's field, you cannot apply the part of the effect that returns your opponent's Spell/Trap Cards to the Deck. As a result, you also cannot Special Summon a Level 4 or lower Spirit monster from your Deck.
Returning from the field to the Deck ❌ is not treated as[3] Destroying

Interaction with:

    Monster card types

  • Extra Deck Monsters
    Efffects that return/shuffle a monster to the Deck can be applied on Extra Deck monsters like a Fusion Monster. In that case, the monster is not returned/shuffled to the Main Deck and is returned to the Extra Deck instead. Furthermore, in that case, since no cards were returned to the Deck, the Deck is not shuffled. This action is still treated as returning/shuffling a monster to the deck[4].
    Example Question:
    If I activate "Pot of Acquisitiveness" by targeting a total of 3 banished Fusion, Synchro, Xyz, and/or Link Monsters, how does it resolve?
    Answer:
    If you activate "Pot of Acquisitiveness" by targeting a total of 3 banished Fusion, Synchro, Xyz, and/or Link Monsters, those monsters are returned to the Extra Deck instead of the Main Deck. Note that since no cards were returned to the Main Deck, it is not shuffled. After that, you will still draw 1 card normally.

    • Other effects

  • Leaves the field
    When a monster on the field is shuffled into the Deck, it is no longer a card on the field, however its effects that activate when it "leaves the field" will not activate[5]. Deck includes both Main and Extra Deck.

2.7.1 Tributes of a Ritual

Tributes for a ritual are sent to the Graveyard as a result of the effect of the Ritual Spell Card.

  • If the activation of the Ritual Spell Card is negated, since the resolution is not performed, the Tributing is not performed either.
  • Unless stated otherwise, you can only Tribute monsters in your possession.
  • If the Ritual Spell says "whose total Levels equal [X]", you must Tribute monsters so that the sum of the Level matches that value exactly. Also, if "whose total Levels equal [X] or more" is written, the sum does not need to be exactly equal as long as it is equal or more.
  • The number of Tributed monsters cannot be higher than necessary.
    Example If "Prediction Ritual" is activated and its effect that says "You must also Tribute monsters from your hand or field whose total Levels equal 9 or more" resolves, you cannot Tribute a Level 9 and a Level 2 monster, as the Levels already add up to 9 or more with only 1 monster. You can Tribute a Level 5 and a Level 6 monster.
  • Monster Tokens on the field can also be Tributed. In that case, Tokens that would be sent to the Graveyard are removed from the Duel.
  • Since Xyz Monsters and Link Monsters do not have a Level, they cannot be used for the tributes of a Ritual Summon.
  • Trap Cards treated as monsters on the field can also be tributed. In that case, the Tributed Trap Cards are sent to the Graveyard.
  • The Tributes for the Ritual Summon are Tributed by an effect, but afterwards the Ritual Summon is performed. For that reason, optional Trigger Effects that read "when it is sent to the Graveyard, you can do [effect]" that can be activated in that timing cannot be activated during that procedure.

2.8.1 "Ghost Mourner & Moonlit Chill"

Ghost Mourner & Moonlit Chill

If your opponent Special Summons a monster(s) face-up (except during the Damage Step): You can discard this card, then target 1 of those face-up monsters; negate its effects until the end of this turn, also if that face-up monster leaves the field this turn, its controller takes damage equal to its original ATK. You can only use this effect of "Ghost Mourner & Moonlit Chill" once per turn.

You can activate the effect of "Ghost Mourner & Moonlit Chill" by targeting a monster that meets at least one of the following[1]:

  • The monster's printed ATK is neither 0 nor "?".
  • The monster's effects are not negated, and it is an Effect Monster or a Gemini Monster.

Examples:

  • Normal Monster: you can activate "Ghost Mourner & Moonlit Chill"'s effect if the monster's printed ATK is neither 0 nor "?".
  • Token: you can activate "Ghost Mourner & Moonlit Chill"'s effect if the printed ATK for the Token, listed on the effect that Summoned it, is neither 0 nor "?".
    For example, an "Adventurer Token" (Summoned by "Rite of Aramesir") has 2000 ATK listed in "Rite of Aramesir"'s text; you can target the "Adventurer Token" with "Ghost Mourner & Moonlit Chill""'s effect.
    On the other hand, a "Primal Being Token" (Summoned by "Nibiru, the Primal Being"'s effect) has "?" ATK listed in "Nibiru, the Primal Being"'s text; you cannot target a "Primal Being Token" with "Ghost Mourner & Moonlit Chill"'s effect.
  • Effect Monster with 0 ATK: you can generally activate "Ghost Mourner & Moonlit Chill"'s effect. However, if the monster's effects are already being negated, such as if it is Special Summoned while "Skill Drain" is already applying, and the monster's printed ATK is 0 or "?", you cannot activate "Ghost Mourner & Moonlit Chill"'s effect.

Interaction with:

    Other effects

  • Change/Take/Give/Switch Control
    The player who takes damage from the effect of "Ghost Mourner & Moonlit Chill" is the one who controls the targeted monster when it leaves the field[2].
    Example Question:
    When my opponent Special Summoned "Blue-Eyes Alternative White Dragon" this turn, I activated the effect of "Ghost Mourner & Moonlit Chill". During the same turn, if that "Blue-Eyes Alternative White Dragon" leaves the field after its control was changed by the effect of "Creature Swap", which player takes damage?
    Answer:
    The player who takes damage from the effect of "Ghost Mourner & Moonlit Chill" is the one who controls the targeted monster when it leaves the field. (In this scenario, since you were the controller of "Blue-Eyes Alternative White Dragon", you take 3000 damage.)
  • Unaffected
    You can use "Ghost Mourner & Moonlit Chill" targeting a monster unaffected by cards effects. In this scenario, the monster's effects won't be negated but the controller of that monster will still take damage when it leaves the field[3].

2.8.2 "Necrovalley"

Necrovalley

All "Gravekeeper's" monsters gain 500 ATK/DEF. Cards in the GY cannot be banished. Negate any card effect that would move a card in the GY to a different place. Negate any card effect that changes Types or Attributes in the GY.

Interaction with:

    Texts that are not an effect

  • Text that determines Materials and Summoning procedures
    If you Special Summon a Special Summon Monster from the Graveyard using the procedure described in its card text, this is not treated as a Special Summon performed by a card effect. Therefore, since this is not a card effect being applied to cards in the Graveyard (as described in "Necrovalley"'s text), you can Special Summon such monsters this way[1].
    Example Question:
    "Necrovalley" is face-up in my opponent's Field Zone, and its effect is applying.
    In this situation, can I Special Summon "Infernoid Onuncu" using the "by banishing 3 "Infernoid" monsters from your hand or Graveyard" procedure listed in its text?
    Answer:
    If you Special Summon a Special Summon Monster using the procedure described in its card text, this is not treated as a Special Summon performed by a card effect. Therefore, since this is not a card effect being applied to cards in the Graveyard (as described in "Necrovalley"'s text), you can Special Summon "Infernoid Onuncu" this way.
    However, note that the effect of "Necrovalley" that prevents players from banishing cards from the Graveyard is also applying. This prevents you from banishing cards from the Graveyard, regardless of whether a card effect is doing so.
    Therefore, in order to Special Summon "Infernoid Onuncu", you will need to banish 3 "Infernoid" monsters from your hand.

    • Other effects

  • During the [X] Phase, do [A] - Delayed effects
    An effect that resolves at a later point, moving a card(s) in the GY to a different place, can be activated when "Necrovalley" is face-up on the field and its effect will not be negated. If at the time of the delayed effect resolution "Necrovalley" is still face-up on the field, that effect won't resolve successfully and won't move any card in the GY. If on the other hand "Necrovalley" is not face-up on the field anymore, then that effect resolves normally by moving a card(s) in the GY[2].
    Example Question:
    "Necrovalley" is face-up in my opponent's Field Zone. In this situation, if I Normal or Special Summon "Harpie Oracle" and activate its effect, will it be negated?
    Answer:
    Even if "Harpie Oracle" is Normal or Special Summoned and activates its effect while the effect of "Necrovalley" is applying, as in this scenario, the effect will not be negated.
    However, if the effect of "Necrovalley" is applying during the End Phase when you apply "Harpie Oracle"'s effect, you cannot add an appropriate Spell/Trap Card from your Graveyard to your hand.
    (If the effect of "Necrovalley" has stopped applying by the time you reach the End Phase, you can add the Spell/Trap Card from your Graveyard to your hand normally.)
